# Amazon Relational Database Service Getting Started Guide

- [Additional resources](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/additional-resources.html)
- [Document history](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/doc-history.html)

## [Getting started with Amazon RDS](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/what-is-rds.html)

- [Key concepts](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/concepts.html): Amazon RDS is a fully managed service that simplifies the deployment and management of relational databases in the cloud.
- [Personas](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/personas.html): This guide is structured around common Amazon RDS user roles to provide targeted, actionable guidance based on your responsibilities and objectives.
- [Setting up](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/setting-up.html): Learn how to set up your AWS account in order to use Amazon RDS.


## [Creating a DB instance](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/creating.html)

- [Choosing your database engine](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/choosing-engine.html): Receive guidance on how to choose a database engine in Amazon RDS that is suitable for your needs.
- [Creating your first DB instance](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/create-instance-overview.html): Create your first DB instance in Amazon RDS, choose the appropriate allocated storage, and select an instance class that aligns with your use case.


## [Securing your DB instance](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/security.html)

- [Setting up public or private access](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/security-public-private.html): When you create an Amazon RDS DB instance, one of the most important decisions you make is determining how others can access it.
- [Determining who can connect](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/security-groups.html): Security groups act as virtual firewalls for your Amazon RDS DB instance, controlling inbound and outbound traffic.
- [Database authentication options](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/database-auth.html): Authentication determines how users and applications connect to your Amazon RDS database.


## [Connecting to your DB instance](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/connecting.html)

- [Using the console to retrieve connection information](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/connecting-console.html): Before you can connect to your Amazon RDS DB instance, you need to gather the connection details, including the endpoint, port, and other required settings.
- [Using the AWS CLI to retrieve connection information](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/connecting-cli.html): While the AWS Command Line Interface (AWS CLI) doesn't directly connect to a database for querying or interactive use, it provides tools to manage and test the connectivity of your Amazon RDS DB instance.
- [Connecting using a database client](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/connecting-client.html): Database clients provide a user-friendly way to connect to and manage your Amazon RDS DB instance.
- [Troubleshooting connection issues](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/connecting-troubleshooting.html): When you attempt to connect to an Amazon RDS DB instance, you might encounter common issues that prevent successful connections.


## [Managing your DB instance](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/managing.html)

- [Backing up and restoring](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/managing-backup-restore.html): Data backups are important for recovering from accidental deletions, corruption, or unexpected failures.
- [Monitoring](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/managing-monitoring-perf.html): Effective monitoring is essential to maintain the health and performance of your Amazon RDS DB instance.


## [Optimizing and scaling](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/advanced.html)

- [Scaling and high availability](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/scaling-ha.html): Scaling and high availability are important aspects of managing your Amazon RDS DB instance to meet changing application requirements.
- [Advanced security options](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonRDS/latest/gettingstartedguide/advanced-security.html): It's important to secure your Amazon RDS DB instance to protecting your data and ensuring compliance with organizational and regulatory standards.
