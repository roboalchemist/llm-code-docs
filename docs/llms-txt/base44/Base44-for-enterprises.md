### Scalable workspace

The workspace gives you a central place to manage everything your organization needs. Access all your apps and members in one view, track roles, app access, seat assignments, and billing status.

You can invite users individually or in bulk, assign or revoke seats instantly, and adapt your setup with flexible seat allocation. The workspace supports large teams and complex permission structures, making it easy to see usage and access details at a glance as your business grows.

To access your organization's workspace, click your profile icon at the top right of Base44. Then hover over your organization's workspace and click **Manage**.

### SSO for workspace access

Keep your workspace secure and streamline onboarding by connecting Base44 with your organization’s trusted identity provider, such as Google or Azure. Single Sign-On (SSO) allows every team member to access Base44 using your company login, reducing the hassle of password management and protecting company data behind a single, secure sign-in process.

When SSO is enabled, your organization controls who can join the workspace, and new members with your approved email domain are automatically added with a secure viewer role.

### SSO for app access

Enterprise SSO for app access means your team can sign in to every app in your workspace with a single set of company credentials. This approach eliminates the need to set up and remember separate logins for each app, reducing password fatigue and the risk of unauthorized access.

With SSO in place, company-wide security policies are enforced automatically, ensuring consistent protection for every app as your workspace grows. Centralized sign-on also makes it easier for admins to control access and respond quickly if changes are needed.

<Warning>
  Only enterprise admins can manage SSO for app access. Once enabled, app users will use your organization’s SSO credentials to access every app in the workspace.
</Warning>

### App visibility

App visibility management lets you define who can access apps in your enterprise workspace and how broadly they are shared from the start. By setting default visibility, such as private, workspace-only, or public, you ensure every new app meets your organization’s security and collaboration needs.

This helps your workspace stay compliant, keeps sensitive information confidential, and avoids accidental exposure of business data. Admins can also grant or restrict the ability for members to make apps public, aligning controls with company policies and oversight requirements.

### IP allowlist

IP allowlists help you protect sensitive data in your enterprise workspace by limiting access to specific, trusted networks. You decide which IP addresses can reach your workspace and its apps, so only people connecting from approved office networks or VPN endpoints can sign in. This reduces the risk of unauthorized access from unknown locations and keeps your security posture aligned with your company’s network policies.

<Frame>
    <img src="https://mintcdn.com/base44/RnJawmMgQjm78wjx/images/2025-12-28_13-58-06.png?fit=max&auto=format&n=RnJawmMgQjm78wjx&q=85&s=6c097893b4b2f33250338a44301e15c1" alt="2025 12 28 13 58 06" width="1047" height="858" data-path="images/2025-12-28_13-58-06.png" />

When IP allowlists are enabled, all apps in the workspace are covered, including both published apps and apps in builder mode. This means your team can safely build and manage sensitive workflows while you keep tight control over where connections come from. You can allow single IPs, ranges, or subnets, which makes it easy to reflect your corporate network design and update it as your infrastructure changes.
