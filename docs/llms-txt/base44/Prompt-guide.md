## The Prompt Formula

"Create a \[Function] website. Use a \[Layout] structure. Apply a \[Visual Style] aesthetic."

### Step 1 | Define the function

Tell the AI exactly what "job" the website is doing. A portfolio needs to be bold, while a bank needs to look secure.

* **SaaS Landing Page:** Focused on conversion, features, and trust.
* **Creative Portfolio:** Focused on personality, visuals, and the "wow" factor.
* **E-Commerce Store:** Focused on product grids, clarity, and speed.
* **Editorial or Blog:** Focused on readability and typography.

### Step 2 | Choose the visual style

Describe the specific vibe you want. This goes beyond "clean" or "modern."

* **Tech and trust:** Glassmorphism (frosted glass) or Aurora Gradients (soft light).
* **Luxury and story:** Dark Academia (moody, serif fonts), Eco-Minimalism (earth tones), or Monochrome (black and white).
* **Creative and bold:** Neo-Brutalism (raw outlines, clashing colors), Y2K, or Vaporwave (retro aesthetics).

### Step 3 | Select the layout

Tell the AI how to organize the content on the screen so it doesn't have to guess.

* **The Bento Grid:** Everything is organized into distinct boxes or cells. This is best for feature-heavy sites.
* **Scrollytelling:** The site is one long canvas. As you scroll, elements assemble or fade in. This is best for story-heavy sites.
* **Split-Screen:** The screen is cut in half. One side is sticky (text), while the other side moves (images). This is best for product-heavy sites.

## What to Avoid

To ensure the AI understands your vision clearly, avoid these common prompting mistakes:

* **Vague requests:** Comments like "Make it better" or "Fix it" without context leave the AI guessing. Be specific about what needs to change.
* **Too much at once:** "Build a full e-commerce site with payments, inventory, reviews, and analytics" in one message can cause confusion. Start with the core structure and add features step-by-step.
* **Assuming the AI remembers everything:** If you talked with the AI days ago or in a different chat, remind it of the context.
* **Expecting the AI to read your mind:** If you have a specific design flow or layout in mind, describe it as accurately as you can.

## Tips for great prompts

These 4 simple strategies will help you write prompts that give you the strongest results with Base44.

<Card title="Be clear and specific" icon="megaphone" iconType="regular" color="#FF5500">
  The more details you share, the better the results. You can define additional features later, but start with the most important ones. To help you clearly define the **Function** (Step 1 of the formula above), try the simple Who, What, Why framework:

  * **WHO:** Who is this app for? (e.g., Independent consultants)
  * **WHAT:** What does it help them do? (e.g., Manage client projects and deadlines)
  * **WHY:** Why would someone use it? (e.g., To save time and stay organized)

  With a clear prompt like this, Base44 can quickly turn your idea into a working app.
</Card>

<Card title="Think about who will use your app" icon="head-side-gear" iconType="regular" color="#FF5500">
  Picture how you, or someone else will use your app. Describe the main steps or journey your users will take, such as signing up, adding information, or tracking progress. This helps the AI design an experience that feels natural and helpful.

  For example, you can tell the AI "Users sign up, create a new project, set milestones, and track their progress daily.”
</Card>

<Card title="Focus on the outcome, not the process" icon="microchip-ai" iconType="regular" color="#FF5500">
  You do not need to explain any technical steps or describe how things should work behind the scenes.

  Simply share the end result you want, like “Let teachers upload assignments and send feedback to students.” The AI handles all the complex details for you.
</Card>

<Card title="Keep evolving your app" icon="arrow-trend-up" iconType="regular" color="#FF5500">
  Your first prompt is just the beginning. You can always ask for changes, updates, or new features.

  For bigger or more complex apps, try building in steps. Start simple, then add features or design details one at a time. Experiment until your app matches your vision. Every time you give feedback, Base44 will keep improving your app.
</Card>

## Techniques for better results

When you’re refining your app, there are some ways you can talk to Base44 that will help it execute on your vision.

| **Technique**                         | **When to Use It**                                     | **Examples**                                                                                                  | **Pro Tip**                                                                                                                                       |
| :------------------------------------ | :----------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **“Make it more…” / “Make it less…”** | Adjust tone, layout, or emphasis                       | - “Make the header less bold.”<br />- “Make the CTA more persuasive.”                                         | <u>Add </u>*<u>why</u>*: “Make the CTA more persuasive because it’s the final step in the user’s journey.”                                        |
| **“Add a…” / “Remove the…”**          | Add/remove specific features or UI blocks              | - “Add a comments section to each task.”<br />- “Remove the pricing page for now.”                            | <u>Group actions for greater efficiency</u>: “Add a comments section and move it below the task list.”                                            |
| **“Change \[this] to \[that]”**       | Adjust text, visuals, layout, or component logic       | - “Change the sidebar color to charcoal.”<br />- “Change the ‘Submit’ button to say ‘Send Request’.”          | <u>Layer context</u>: “Change the button text to match the friendly tone used in the rest of the app.”                                            |
| **“It should feel like…”**            | Borrow familiar app styles to guide layout or behavior | - “It should feel like a mix between Duolingo and Habitica.”<br />- “Like a lighter version of Airtable.”     | <u>Clarify </u>*<u>which parts</u>*: “Use Duolingo’s playful tone and Habitica’s gamified progress system.”                                       |
| **“Add logic for…”***(optional)*      | Add functional rules or flows without needing code     | - “Add logic to hide completed items from the default view.”<br />- “Send a reminder if no action in 3 days.” | <u>Describe the desired outcome once the logic is added</u>: “Add logic so users get nudged if they haven’t logged in for a week.”                |
| **“Group or organize...”**            | Structure content for clarity or workflow              | - “Group tasks by category”<br />- “Organize settings into collapsible sections”                              | <u>Mention user needs</u>: “Organize tasks by category so users can quickly find what they need.”                                                 |
| **“Add conditional behavior…”**       | Introduce smart branching or state-based functionality | - “Only show the download button after payment is confirmed.”                                                 | <u>Start simple, expand later</u>: “Add the condition first, then I’ll add variations per user type.”                                             |
| **“Let users…” statements**           | Frame functionality from the end-user’s perspective    | - “Let users upload documents and tag them.”<br />- “Let users bookmark resources for later.”                 | <u>Frame the functionality around unlocking value for the user</u>: “Let users upload documents and tag them so they can easily find them later.” |

## Chatting with AI

Building your app is a conversation, not a one-time command. Once you have used the techniques above to refine your vision, you can use the chat interface to shape your app step by step.

You can ask questions, add new features, update details, or change your mind at any time. There’s no limit to how many changes you can make. If you want to try a new layout, add a page, or remove something, simply type your new request and the AI will update your app in seconds.

You also have tools to help you along the way:

* **Discuss mode:** Use Discuss mode whenever you want to brainstorm or talk through ideas with the AI before making any changes. It’s a great way to explore options and get feedback without affecting your live app until you’re ready. [Learn more about the chat modes](https://docs.base44.com/Building-your-app/AI-chat-modes)
* **Revert history:** If you change your mind or want to go back, you can easily restore any previous version of your app. Experiment as much as you like, knowing you can always undo or revisit past versions if you need to. [Learn more about reverting changes](https://docs.base44.com/Building-your-app/AI-chat-modes#reverting-changes)

It’s ideal for making sure your goals are clear and exploring alternatives before applying updates to your app.  [Learn more about the chat modes](https://docs.base44.com/Building-your-app/AI-chat-modes)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why isn’t the AI following my instructions correctly?">
    It make take the AI a few attempts to get the perfect solution. The intent isn’t always fully clear and small bugs may pop up. To get better results from the chat, it’s really helpful to be as specific as possible about the element you’re referring to.

    Here are some tips we recommend:

    * Using Visual Edits to directly highlight the element and describe the change you want
    * Explaining what you do want to happen (rather than what’s wrong)
    * If repeating prompts, try rephrasing with keywords like redesign or rebuild
    * Writing in your own natural language, the simpler and clearer the request, the more accurate the response will be.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What should I do if AI responses are slow or get stuck?">
    If the AI is taking too long or seems stuck, try refreshing the page or resending your prompt. This usually fixes the issue and gets things moving again.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How can I prompt better to avoid losing code changes with AI-generated code?">
    When working with AI-generated code, results may not always be as expected. To reduce the risk of losing your changes:

    * Use AI controls to add custom instructions or important notes you want the AI to remember for every prompt.
    * Repeat any critical details or requirements in your prompts, especially when context matters.
    * Save your work regularly and keep backup copies of important versions of your code.
    * Remember that effective prompting is a skill. As you gain experience and AI tools improve, your results will get better.
    * Carefully review both your prompts and the AI’s output to make sure your context and requirements are handled the way you intend.
  </Accordion>
