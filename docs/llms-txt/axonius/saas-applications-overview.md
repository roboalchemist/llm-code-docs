# SaaS Applications

Axonius **SaaS Applications** provides full visibility across your entire SaaS landscape to build a complete inventory, mitigate critical risk areas, and optimize usage and spend. It ensures that every application – managed or unmanaged – is discovered and fully optimized for risk, performance, and cost.
This allows for you to respond to issues in real-time - block unmanaged risky applications, offload users that are not using approved applications, right-size your licenses, and much more.

**SaaS Applications** enables users to take control of their SaaS stack by identifying risky apps and access patterns. Axonius uncovers shadow apps, unmanaged extensions, OAuth tokens, and users operating outside approved authentication protocols.

See the following to get started with SaaS Applications:

* [**SaaS Applications Discovery**](/docs/saas-applications) - How many SaaS Applications are used in your organization and how Axonius discovers them. View a breakdown of your SaaS Applications by their risk score, their Approved vs Unapproved status, and which applications are Shadow SaaS Applications.
* [**User Initiated Extensions**](/docs/user-initiated-extensions) - Review and understand the access permissions other applications have for your SaaS applications, and how to identify which permissions are excessive and potentially risky.
* [**SSPM - SaaS Application Settings**](/docs/application-settings) - Tools for reviewing SaaS application settings data retrieved from Axonius adapters and see how to find and fix risky misconfigurations.
* [**SaaS Application Extensions**](/docs/application-extensions) - Applications that are integrated with your SaaS applications by viewing their extension configurations, including [Admin Managed Extensions](/docs/admin-managed-extensions),[User Initiated Extensions](/docs/user-initiated-extensions), and [Application Add-ons](/docs/application-add-ons). This can allow you to identify which of the integrations are active or inactive, how many integrations are configured for each of your applications, and the number of integrations for each user in your organization. You can also control application authentication and authorization, allow users to configure how they access their applications, and more.
* [**SaaS Licenses**](/docs/licenses) - View all licenses for SaaS applications detected in your organization. Learn how to increase management and oversight over the various SaaS licenses used in your organization.
* [**SaaS Expenses**](/docs/expenses) - Learn how to identify redundant SaaS applications, prevent hidden costs, and other ways to optimize the SaaS spend in your organization.
* [**SaaS Activity**](/docs/activities) - Learn how to review user and entity activity in your SaaS environment to detect suspicious behavior, reduce risk, and maintain strong security for your organization.
