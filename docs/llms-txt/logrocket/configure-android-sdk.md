# Configure Android SDK

Getting started is easy:

1. **Connect your app**\
   Visit [https://app.logrocket.com](https://app.logrocket.com) to create an app ID - this links your app to your LogRocket workspace

2. **Add the SDK**\
   [Initialize the SDK](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/android) in your application to start recording sessions

3. **Protect sensitive data**\
   Sanitize your data before sending it to LogRocket. Control exactly what LogRocket records - easily exclude [UI elements](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/android-redact-view), [text](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/android-automatically-sanitize-text), or [network data ](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/android-capturing-network-traffic) that shouldn't appear in session replays

4. **Capture network activity**\
   The LogRocket Android SDK does not automatically capture Network Requests. [Enable network capture](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/android-capturing-network-traffic) to see network requests and responses in session replay.

Get the most out of LogRocket -- follow the steps below to unlock deeper insights and make your session replays even more valuable

1. **Identify Users**\
   [Associate sessions with known users](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/android-identify) for easier search and enhanced analytics

2. **See full stack traces**\
   [Upload](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/stack-traces-android) mapping files for full stack trace visibility - no more guessing which line failed

3. **Capture WebViews**\
   If you use WebViews in your app, follow [initialize LogRocket on WebViews](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/webviews-on-android) to ensure every page view appears in session replay

4. **Tag key pages**\
   By default, LogRocket captures Activity events for navigation tracking - but these don't always reflect every screen you'd consider a "page". Add [custom page tags](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/capture-custom-pages-android) to to make your analytics more meaningful, highlight real user journeys, and pinpoint where users drop off

5. **Upload custom fonts**\
   If you use custom fonts or icons, [upload custom fonts ](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/android-capture-custom-fonts)to LogRocket so you see exactly what your users see

6. **Track custom events**\
   While LogRocket automatically captures standard events like clicks and navigation, you can [define custom events](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/android-custom-events) to capture anything unique to your app, such as purchases, signups, or anything tied to your core KPIs.

7. **Log custom errors**\
   Record custom [errors](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/android-capture-error) and [exceptions](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/android-capture-exceptions) for maximum visibility in LogRocket
