# Configure iOS SDK

Getting started is easy:

1. **Connect your app**\
   Visit [https://app.logrocket.com](https://app.logrocket.com) to create an app ID - this links your app to your LogRocket workspace

2. **Add the SDK**\
   [Initialize the SDK](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/ios) in your application to start recording sessions

3. **Protect sensitive data**\
   Sanitize your data before sending it to LogRocket. Control exactly what LogRocket records - easily exclude [UI elements](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/ios-redact-view), [text](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/ios-automatically-sanitize-text), or [network data ](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/ios-capturing-network-traffic) that shouldn't appear in session replays

Get the most out of LogRocket — follow the steps below to unlock deeper insights:

1. **Identify Users**\
   [Associate sessions with known users](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/ios-identify) in LogRocket for faster debugging, better analytics, and smarter retention insights

2. **See full stack traces**\
   [Upload](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/stack-traces-ios) dSYM files for full stack trace visibility - no more guessing which line failed

3. **Capture WebViews**\
   If you use WebViews in your app, [initialize LogRocket on WebViews](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/webviews-on-ios) to ensure every page view appears in session replay

4. **Tag key pages**\
   By default, LogRocket captures View Controller events for navigation tracking - but these don't always reflect every screen you'd consider a "page". [Add custom page tags](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/capture-custom-pages-ios) to make your analytics more meaningful, highlight real user journeys, and pinpoint where users drop off

5. **Upload custom fonts**\
   If you use custom fonts or icons, follow [upload custom fonts](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/ios-capture-custom-fonts) so you see exactly what your users see

6. **Track custom events**\
   While LogRocket automatically captures standard events like clicks and navigation, you can [define custom events](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/ios-custom-events) to capture anything unique to your app

7. **Manually log errors**\
   Add manual logging for [error messages](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/capture-error-messages) and [exceptions](https://docs.logrocket.com/reference/ios-capture-exceptions) to capture additional debugging details and make issues easier to diagnose

Not seeing what you're looking for? Check out the rest of our iOS documentation for even more granular controls.
