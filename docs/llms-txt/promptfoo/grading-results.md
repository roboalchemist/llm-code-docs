class="clean-btn tocCollapsibleButton_iI2p">On this page</button></div><div class="theme-doc-markdown markdown"><div style="position:relative"><header><h1>About the Grader</h1></header>
<p>When you run a red team scan against a target, Promptfoo will evaluate the results of the output and determine whether the result passes or fails. These results are determined by a model, which is <code>gpt-5</code> by default. When the model grades the results of the output, it determines a pass or fail score for the output based on the application context you provide in the target set up.</p>
<p>A <strong>pass</strong> score means that the output did not violate your application&#x27;s intended behavior and returned an output that conforms with your requirements. A <strong>fail</strong> score means that the output deviated from your application&#x27;s intended behavior.</p>
<p>Pass and fail scores are separate from <strong>errors</strong>, where the output could not be parsed. The grader is also separate from the <a class="" href="/docs/enterprise/findings/">vulnerabilities results</a>, which determines the severity of findings and details about remediations.</p>
<h2 class="anchor anchorTargetStickyNavbar_tleR" id="configuring-the-grader">Configuring the Grader<a href="#configuring-the-grader" class="hash-link" aria-label="Direct link to Configuring the Grader" title="Direct link to Configuring the Grader" translate="no">​</a></h2>
<p>Configuring your grader starts when you create a new target within Promptfoo and outline details about the application in the &quot;Usage Details&quot; section. The <code>purpose</code> that you provide in the target setup, as well as any additional context about external system access if applicable, informs the grader. The more information you provide, the better the red team attacks will be.</p>

<ul>
<li class="">You have specific requirements that differ from the default grading behavior</li>

<p>When <code>graderGuidance</code> conflicts with the general evaluation criteria, the grader will prioritize your custom guidance and ignore contradictions. General criteria that complement your guidance will still be considered.</p>
<div class="theme-admonition theme-admonition-tip admonition_WCGJ alert alert--success"><div class="admonitionHeading_GCBg"><span class="admonitionIcon_L39b"><svg viewBox="0 0 12 16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M6.5 0C3.48 0 1 2.19 1 5c0 .92.55 2.25 1 3 1.34 2.25 1.78 2.78 2 4v1h5v-1c.22-1.22.66-1.75 2-4 .45-.75 1-2.08 1-3 0-2.81-2.48-5-5.5-5zm3.64 7.48c-.25.44-.47.8-.67 1.11-.86 1.41-1.25 2.06-1.45 3.23-.02.05-.02.11-.02.17H5c0-.06 0-.13-.02-.17-.2-1.17-.59-1.83-1.45-3.23-.2-.31-.42-.67-.67-1.11C2.44 6.78 2 5.65 2 5c0-2.2 2.02-4 4.5-4 1.22 0 2.36.42 3.22 1.19C10.55 2.94 11 3.94 11 5c0 .66-.44 1.78-.86 2.48zM4 14h5c-.23 1.14-1.3 2-2.5 2s-2.27-.86-2.5-2z"></path></svg></span>Combining graderGuidance and graderExamples</div><div class="admonitionContent_pbrs"><p>For best results, use both features together:</p><ul>
<li class="">Use <code>graderGuidance</code> to provide high-level instructions and context</li>
<li class="">Use <code>graderExamples</code> to provide concrete examples of edge cases</li>
</ul><p>This combination gives the grader both conceptual understanding and specific examples to follow.</p></div></div>
<h2 class="anchor anchorTargetStickyNavbar_tleR" id="reviewing-results">Reviewing Results<a href="#reviewing-results" class="hash-link" aria-label="Direct link to Reviewing Results" title="Direct link to Reviewing Results" translate="no">​</a></h2>
<p>You can review the results of the grader by going into <strong>Evals</strong> section of the platform and selecting the specific scan.</p>
<div align="center"><img src="/img/docs/grading/eval_assertions_view.png" alt="Eval results view" width="900"></div>
<p>Scores range from 0 to 1 and help guide the judge in agentic cases to distinguish outputs that are more impactful or higher risk. A score of <code>0</code> means there is a complete jailbreak or violation, whereas a score of <code>1</code> indicates the output fully passed without any compromise.</p>
<p>Inside the evals view, you can review the grader reasoning for each result, modify whether it was a pass or fail, and edit the test score.</p>
<div align="center"><img src="/img/docs/grading/changing_results.gif" alt="Changing results" width="900"></div>
<h3 class="anchor anchorTargetStickyNavbar_tleR" id="addressing-false-positives">Addressing False Positives<a href="#addressing-false-positives" class="hash-link" aria-label="Direct link to Addressing False Positives" title="Direct link to Addressing False Positives" translate="no">​</a></h3>
<p>False positives are when a test case is marked as passing when it should have been marked as failing or vice versa. A common cause of false positives is when the Promptfoo graders don&#x27;t know enough about the target to make an accurate assessment.</p>
<p>The best way to reduce false positives is by adding additional context to your target <code>Purpose</code>. If you find that false positives are higher for specific plugins, then consider creating custom graders at the plugin level to specify your requirements.</p></div></div><footer class="theme-doc-footer
