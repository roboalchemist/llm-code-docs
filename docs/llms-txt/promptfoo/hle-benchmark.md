<p>HLE addresses benchmark saturation - the phenomenon where advanced models achieve over 90% accuracy on existing tests like MMLU, making it difficult to measure continued progress. HLE provides a more challenging eval for current AI systems.</p>

<ul>
<li class="">Created by 1,000+ PhD-level experts across 500+ institutions</li>
<li class="">Covers 100+ subjects from mathematics to humanities</li>
<li class="">14% of questions include images alongside text</li>
<li class="">Questions resist simple web search solutions</li>
<li class="">Focuses on verifiable, closed-ended problems</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="anchor anchorTargetStickyNavbar_tleR" id="eval-limitations">Eval Limitations<a href="#eval-limitations" class="hash-link" aria-label="Direct link to Eval Limitations" title="Direct link to Eval Limitations" translate="no">​</a></h2>
<p>Keep in mind these results are preliminary - we only tested 50 questions per model in a single run. That&#x27;s a pretty small sample from HLE&#x27;s 14,000+ questions, and we didn&#x27;t optimize our approach much (token budgets, prompts, etc. were chosen somewhat arbitrarily).</p>
<p>o4-mini&#x27;s 42% success rate stands out and requires validation through larger samples and multiple runs. Performance will likely vary considerably across different subjects and question formats.</p>
<h2 class="anchor anchorTargetStickyNavbar_tleR" id="implications-for-ai-development">Implications for AI Development<a href="#implications-for-ai-development" class="hash-link" aria-label="Direct link to Implications for AI Development" title="Direct link to Implications for AI Development" translate="no">​</a></h2>
<p>HLE provides a useful benchmark for measuring AI progress on academic tasks. The low current scores indicate significant room for improvement in AI reasoning capabilities.</p>
<p>As Dan Hendrycks (CAIS co-founder) notes:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>&quot;When I released the MATH benchmark in 2021, the best model scored less than 10%; few predicted that scores higher than 90% would be achieved just three years later. Right now, Humanity&#x27;s Last Exam shows there are still expert questions models cannot answer. We will see how long that lasts.&quot;</p>
</blockquote>
<p><strong>Key findings:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li class="">Current reasoning models achieve modest performance on HLE questions</li>
<li class="">Success varies significantly by domain and question type</li>
<li class="">Token budget increases alone don&#x27;t guarantee accuracy improvements</li>
<li class="">Substantial gaps remain between AI and human expert performance</li>
</ul>
<p>Promptfoo provides HLE eval capabilities through automated dataset integration, parallel execution, and comprehensive results analysis.</p>
