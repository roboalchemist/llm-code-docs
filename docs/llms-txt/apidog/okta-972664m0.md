# Okta

To configure SCIM with Okta for your organization, you must have administrator access for both Okta and Apidog.

## Preparation

Before configuring in the Okta admin console, please navigate to the **SAML SSO** page in **Apidog organization settings**. Click the **Generate SCIM Token** button and keep this page open during the following steps.

## Modifying SSO Configuration

To make SCIM work properly, you need to modify the SSO **Application username format**:

1. Open the Okta admin console in a browser.
2. Go to **Applications** and select the corresponding app.
3. Under the **Sign On** tab, click **Edit** button in the **Settings** tab.
4. In the **Credentials Details**:
   - Set **Application username format** to **Custom**.
   - Enter the following expression: `user.getInternalProperty("id")`.

## Configuring SCIM in Okta

Follow these steps to set up SCIM:

1. Open the Okta admin console in a browser.
2. Go to **Applications** and click the corresponding app. Under the **General** tab, click the **Edit** button in the **App Settings** section. Change the **Provisioning** option from **None** to **SCIM**.
3. After saving, a new **Provisioning** tab will appear next to **Sign On** tap. Click on it, then click **Edit** in the **SCIM Connection** section.
4. Copy Apidog's **SCIM API Endpoint URL** and paste it into Okta's **SCIM connector base URL** field.
5. For **Unique identifier field for users** in Okta: enter `userName`.
6. For **Supported provisioning actions** in Okta, check **Import New Users**, **Profile Updates** and **Push New Users**.
7. For **Authentication Mode**, select HTTP Header.
8. Copy Apidog's **SCIM Token** and paste it into Okta's **Authorization** field.
9. After completing the configuration, click **Test Connector Configuration** in Okta. If you see checkmarks next to **User Import**, **Import Profile Updates**, and **Create Users**, the setup is successful. Close the test window and save the configurations.
10. In the Okta App's **Provisioning Settings** tab under the **To App** section, click **Edit** in the **Provisioning to App** section, and enable **Create Users** by checking **Enable**.

## Testing Your SCIM

Go back to Apidog, where you will see a list of inactive users.

- Once these inactive members log in using SSO, their status will change to **active**, and they will occupy paid seats.
- Users in an inactive state do not occupy any paid seats.
