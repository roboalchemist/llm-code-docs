## [Channel Management](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/channel-management.html)

### [Program management accounts](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/program-management-accounts.html)

A program management account (PMA) is an AWS management account that you use to manage your AWS Channel Program participation and relationships with customer or distribution seller AWS accounts.

- [Creating a program management account](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/creating-pma.html): To create a program management account, you will need an AWS management account ID and an active channel program registration.
- [Activating a program management account](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/activating-pma.html): To activate a PMA, a channel handshake must be accepted by the AWS management account.

### [Channel relationships](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/channel-relationships.html)

Channel relationships in AWS Partner Central provide a centralized way to manage connections between your program management accounts and your customer, distribution seller, or internal AWS Organizations.

- [Creating a relationship](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/creating-relationship.html): When creating a new relationship, you'll need to provide specific information to properly configure the connection:

### [Service periods](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/service-periods.html)

Service periods create mutual agreements that prevent unilateral changes to billing transfers during specified commitment periods.

- [Creating a service period](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/creating-service-period.html): Service periods can be added to any channel relationship using Partner Central Channel Management.
- [Managing an active service period](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/managing-active-service-period.html): Active service periods impact both parties Billing Transfer management experience in Billing and Cost Management console.
- [Early termination](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/early-termination.html): Partners can request early termination of service periods before their natural expiration, but both parties must mutually consent through another channel handshake process.

### [Migrating legacy channel accounts](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/migrating-legacy-channel-accounts.html)

This guide explains how AWS Channel Partners can migrate their existing channel end customers in partner-controlled AWS Organizations to billing transfer, enabling customers to maintain independent AWS Organizations while partners retain billing responsibility.

- [Transferring Organization ownership](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/transferring-organization-ownership.html): This migration path transfers root account ownership of an existing AWS Organization to your customer while maintaining billing responsibility through billing transfer.
- [Transferring member accounts](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/transferring-member-accounts.html): This migration path moves individual member accounts from your existing AWS Organization to a new customer-owned organization, while maintaining billing responsibility through billing transfer.
- [Mapping IAM roles to a channel management user](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/channel-management-user-mapping.html): This section explains how to map AWS Partner Central AWS Identity and Access Management (IAM) roles to your channel management service users on AWS Partner Central.

### [MAP Partner Performance Index (PPI)](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/map-partner-performance-index.html)

Learn how to understand and use the MAP Partner Performance Index (PPI) to track your organization's effectiveness in delivering Migration Acceleration Program (MAP) projects and improve your migration practice.

- [What is the MAP Partner Performance Index (PPI)?](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/ppi-overview.html): The MAP Partner Performance Index (PPI) is a comprehensive measurement system that evaluates partner effectiveness in delivering successful AWS migration projects where partners have requested investments from AWS.
- [Understanding your PPI score](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/ppi-scoring.html): Your PPI score is a single percentage that reflects your overall effectiveness across four key performance areas.

### [The four key performance metrics](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/ppi-key-metrics.html)

This section provides detailed information about each of the four metrics used to calculate your PPI score.

- [Metric 1: Assess Conversion Rate](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/ppi-metric-assess-conversion.html): What it measures: Your ability to convert AWS-funded Assess projects into signed migration commitments.
- [Metric 2: Migration Delivery Velocity Health](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/ppi-metric-speed-value.html): What it measures: How quickly your migration projects begin generating meaningful AWS revenue after the project reached $1k in tagged spend.
- [Metric 3: Final Migration Revenue Realization on Completed Projects at Portfolio Level](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/ppi-metric-portfolio-success.html): What it measures: Your overall effectiveness in delivering expected revenue outcomes across all completed migration projects.
- [Metric 4: Final Migration Revenue Realization on Completed Projects at Individual Deal Level](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/ppi-metric-project-success.html): What it measures: The consistency of your project delivery by examining individual project outcomes.

### [Performance tiers and benefits](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/ppi-tiers-benefits.html)

The PPI system recognizes different levels of partner capability and provides appropriate support and benefits for each tier.

- [High Performance Track (67% - 100%)](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/ppi-tier-high-performance.html): Who qualifies: Partners delivering exceptional results across all four metrics, or strong performance in most areas.
- [Core Program Track (50% - 66%)](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/ppi-tier-core-program.html): Who qualifies: Partners meeting core expectations with solid execution and clear growth potential.
- [Turnaround Track (33% - 49%)](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/partner-central/latest/getting-started/ppi-tier-turnaround.html): Who qualifies: Partners who need focused support to meet performance standards.
