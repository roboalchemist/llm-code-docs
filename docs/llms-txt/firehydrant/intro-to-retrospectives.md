# Intro to Retrospectives

FireHydrant's retrospectives feature helps teams facilitate better incident reviews with a flexible template system, collaborative editing, and integrated incident data. From customizable questions to contributing factors analysis, retrospectives make it easier to learn from incidents and prevent future occurrences.

## Features at a Glance

### 📋 [Retrospective Templates](https://docs.firehydrant.com/docs/retrospective-templates)

Create and manage multiple templates to standardize your post-incident review process across different types of incidents or teams.

Once created, you can attach one or multiple templates to incidents either [automatically via Runbooks](https://docs.firehydrant.com/docs/runbook-step-add-a-retrospective) or manually from the retrospective view, giving you full flexibility in how you document and learn from incidents.

### 📊 Incident Data

Access all relevant incident information directly within your retrospective, including timeline, contributing factors, and related metrics.

### 📤 Sharing and Export

Share retrospectives with stakeholders through PDF exports, Google Docs, Confluence, or direct links, ensuring everyone stays informed about incident learnings.

## Key Components

### Templates

Templates are the foundation of FireHydrant's retrospective system. They allow you to:

* Define custom sets of questions for different incidents
* Include specific incident data sections
* Attach templates automatically through runbooks or manually

### Retrospective Question Set

**Contributing Factors**

Track and analyze the root causes of incidents:

* Add multiple contributing factors to each retrospective
* Categorize and track patterns across incidents
* Use insights to prevent future occurrences

**Customizable questions**

Capture valuable insights with customizable questions with varying content and types:

* Rich, free-form text (with image support)
* Select/Multi-select fields
* Numeric
* Date
* Read-only text (for instructions, guidance, etc.)

**AI-Drafted Answers/Retros**

FireHydrant's AI Copilot can contextualize data and information from the incident to intelligently answer questions or pre-fill information into your retrospective.

For more information, see [AI-Powered Incident Management](https://docs.firehydrant.com/docs/ai-powered-incident-management).

## Getting Started

<Image align="center" src="https://files.readme.io/dd15cf96a47a0395d12b9dffbfed009753680f9d23ed6274de78e8b18602a78a-retrospectives-getting-started.png" />

1. **Access Retrospectives**: Navigate to any incident, and click the retrospective view button in the top right corner
2. **Choose Templates**:
   * Set up automatic template assignment through runbooks
   * Manually select templates from the retrospective view
3. **Collaborate**: Work together with your team using the new collaborative editing features
4. **Share**: Export to PDF or share direct links with stakeholders

## Best Practices

1. **Template Strategy**: Create specific templates for different incident types or severity levels
2. **Required Fields**: Mark critical questions as required to ensure comprehensive reviews
3. **Contributing Factors**: Add multiple factors to build a complete picture of incident causes
4. **Collaborative Review**: Use the real-time editing features to conduct retrospectives as a team
5. **Timeline Integration**: Reference the incident timeline while completing your retrospective

## Important Notes

* Templates are fixed once applied to an incident. Any changes made to a template after adding it to an incident once will not be reflected, even if the retro is removed and re-added
* Questions cannot be added ad-hoc to an active retrospective
* Your existing retrospective configuration will be preserved in the default template
