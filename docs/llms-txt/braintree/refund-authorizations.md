        The payments industry is moving to an authorization model for refund processing. As per scheme
        rules, refunds should be preceded by authorizations to allow the card issuer to weigh in before

        in real time, to improve transparency.
    </span></p><h2 id="card-networks-affected" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Card networks affected</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#card-networks-affected" aria-label="Card networks affected"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Visa</span></li><li><span>Discover</span></li><li><span>Mastercard</span></li><li><span>Amex</span></li></ul><h2 id="payment-methods-affected" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Payment methods affected</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#payment-methods-affected" aria-label="Payment methods affected"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Credit cards</span></li><li><span>Apple Pay</span></li><li><span>Google Pay</span></li></ul><h2 id="statuses-of-refund-authorizations" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Statuses of refund authorizations</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#statuses-of-refund-authorizations" aria-label="Statuses of refund authorizations"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><h3 id="approved-refunds" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Approved refunds</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#approved-refunds" aria-label="Approved refunds"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        If a refund authorization is approved, the issuer has indicated, in real time, that they will
        accept the refund and deposit it into the cardholder’s account.
    </span></p><h3 id="declined-refunds" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Declined refunds</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#declined-refunds" aria-label="Declined refunds"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        If the refund authorization is declined, the issuer has indicated, in real time, that the
        cardholder’s account is not capable of accepting the refund. A refund decline prevents the
        cardholder’s payment method from being refunded.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>Common reasons for declines:</span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Card account is closed</span></li><li><span>Card account is frozen due to fraud</span></li><li><span>Card account does not support refunds (e.g. some prepaid cards)</span></li></ul><ul class="list list--disc"><div role="alert" class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__1QhIq ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert--note__gRM6J"><div class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__header__T2Wkg"><style data-emotion="css 4757ty-svg-size_xs">.css-4757ty-svg-size_xs{speak:none;display:inline-block;aspect-ratio:1;width:1.125rem;height:1.125rem;}.css-4757ty-svg-size_xs img,.css-4757ty-svg-size_xs svg{width:100%;height:100%;display:block;}</style><span class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__icon__puACD css-4757ty-svg-size_xs" data-ppui-info="icons_8.21.2" role="img" aria-label="note" data-ppui="true"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" fill="currentColor" viewBox="0 0 24 24" width="1em" height="1em" data-ppui="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M21 12a9 9 0 1 1-18 0 9 9 0 0 1 18 0zm-9.662 5h1.32c.18 0 .276-.096.276-.276v-5.925c0-.18-.096-.276-.276-.276h-1.32c-.18 0-.276.096-.276.276v5.925c0 .18.096.276.276.276zM12 9a1 1 0 1 0 0-2 1 1 0 0 0 0 2z" clip-rule="evenodd" data-ppui="true"></path></svg></span><span class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__title__FOcbD">Note</span><span class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__content__2-Ddi"><span>
            Certain declined refund authorization transactions, depending on the network response code, may be force-captured due to high issuer decline rates.
        </span></span></div></div></ul><h2 id="background" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Background</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#background" aria-label="Background"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>Refund authorizations are intended to help solve two industry challenges:</span></p><h3 id="cardholder-transparency-for-refunds" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Cardholder transparency for refunds</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#cardholder-transparency-for-refunds" aria-label="Cardholder transparency for refunds"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Refunds do not immediately appear on cardholder statements, which can cause cardholder confusion
        and frustration. This confusion can lead to increased refund-related customer support inquiries.
    </span></p><h3 id="refund-resolution-timeliness" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Refund resolution timeliness</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#refund-resolution-timeliness" aria-label="Refund resolution timeliness"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Issuers are not required to accept refunds. However, without an authorization framework, it can
        take days for you to be notified after an issuer rejects a refund. Refund delays can increase the
        likelihood of cardholder disputes.
    </span></p><h2 id="benefits" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Benefits</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#benefits" aria-label="Benefits"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>With the introduction of refund authorizations, cardholders can see the following benefits:</span></p><h3 id="improved-cardholder-transparency" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Improved cardholder transparency</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#improved-cardholder-transparency" aria-label="Improved cardholder transparency"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        If a refund authorization is approved by the issuer, the issuer should immediately add a pending
        refund line item to the cardholder’s statement. This pending line item provides real time evidence
        to the cardholder that you’ve initiated the refund process. This transparency is expected to
        reduce refund-related customer support inquiries.
    </span></p><h3 id="real-time-refund-approval-responses" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Real time refund approval responses</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#real-time-refund-approval-responses" aria-label="Real time refund approval responses"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        If the cardholder’s account cannot accept the refund, the issuer will decline the refund
        authorization in real time. This real time response allows you to take immediate action if the
        refund is declined, such as requesting an alternate payment method in order to reattempt their
        refund. This allows you to be more proactive in resolving refund-related issues, which should
        reduce refund-related inquiries.
    </span></p><h3 id="reduction-in-refund-related-disputes" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Reduction in refund-related disputes</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#reduction-in-refund-related-disputes" aria-label="Reduction in refund-related disputes"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        By being more proactive, you can increase the timeliness of refund resolutions, which should
        reduce disputes from cardholders anxious for their refunds.
    </span></p><h2 id="how-to-handle-refund-declines" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>How to handle refund declines</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#how-to-handle-refund-declines" aria-label="How to handle refund declines"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        You should first attempt to refund the original sale transaction via Braintree’s
        </span><a href="/braintree/docs/reference/request/transaction/refund"><span>refund API</span></a><span> or in the
        </span><a href="/braintree/articles/control-panel/transactions/refunds-voids-credits#issuing-a-refund-within-the-control-panel"><span>Control Panel</span></a><span>. If the refund authorization attempt is declined by the issuer, you may refund your customer via
        an alternate method instead.
    </span></p><h3 id="common-alternate-refund-methods" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Common alternate refund methods</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#common-alternate-refund-methods" aria-label="Common alternate refund methods"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Cash</span></li><li><span>Check</span></li><li><span>In-store credit</span></li><li><span>Prepaid card</span></li></ul><h3 id="refund-policies" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Refund policies</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#refund-policies" aria-label="Refund policies"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        It’s important to note that your refund/return policy still allows you to restrict or refuse
        refunds, returns, cancellations, or exchanges, provided that your policy outlines this clearly and
        was properly disclosed to your customer during the original sale transaction.
    </span></p><h2 id="integration-changes" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Integration changes</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#integration-changes" aria-label="Integration changes"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><h3 id="current-refund-workflow" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Current refund workflow</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#current-refund-workflow" aria-label="Current refund workflow"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Braintree’s refund API assumes success for credit card, Apple Pay, and Google Pay-based refunds.
        This means that refund responses indicate the success of the API request itself, but not the
        overall outcome of the refund. After receiving a refund success response, it’s still possible for
        the issuer to reject the refund.
    </span></p><h3 id="future-refund-workflow" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Future refund workflow</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#future-refund-workflow" aria-label="Future refund workflow"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>

        </span><a href="/braintree/docs/reference/request/transaction/refund/all"><span>refund requests</span></a><span> will return

        </span><a href="/braintree/docs/reference/general/processor-responses/authorization-responses"><span>processor responses</span></a><span>, similar to the processor responses returned from sale authorizations. In order to properly

However, without an authorization framework, it can take days for you to be notified after an issuer rejects a refund.
