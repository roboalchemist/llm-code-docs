class="main-article-content"><div class="ecmRichText" style="margin-bottom:0px"><section><h1><span>Disputes</span></h1><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        PayPal disputes can be managed in either your Braintree Control Panel or your PayPal Resolution
        Center. Action taken on either of these platforms will be reflected across the other – including
        any uploaded evidence against disputed transactions.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        This article covers how to manage your PayPal disputes via the Braintree Control Panel. Learn more
        about how to manage this process via the PayPal Resolution Center in
        </span><a class="is-external-link dx-external-href" rel="noopener,noreferrer" href="https://www.paypal.com/us/webapps/mpp/security/resolve-disputes" pa-marked="1" target="_blank"><span>PayPal's documentation</span></a><span>.
    </span></p><h2 id="setup" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Setup</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#setup" aria-label="Setup"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        In order to manage your PayPal disputes via the Braintree Control Panel, you'll need to have a
        full PayPal integration with Braintree. If you're not already processing PayPal transactions
        through Braintree, head back to our
        </span><a href="/braintree/articles/guides/payment-methods/paypal/overview"><span>PayPal Overview</span></a><span> to get
        started.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Once you have added PayPal to your integration and linked your Braintree and PayPal accounts, you
        can then enable PayPal disputes in the Control Panel. In order to do so, your user's role must
        include the Add/Edit Processing Options
        </span><a href="/braintree/articles/control-panel/users-roles/role-permissions#permission-addedit-processing-options-including-avscvv"><span>role permission</span></a><span>. To enable PayPal disputes:
    </span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>Log into the </span><a href="https://www.braintreegateway.com/login"><span>Control Panel</span></a></li><li><span>Click on the gear icon in the top right corner</span></li><li><span>Click </span><strong><span>Account Settings</span></strong><span> from the drop-down menu</span></li><li><span>Scroll to the </span><strong><span>Payment Methods</span></strong><span> section</span></li><li><span>Next to </span><strong><span>PayPal</span></strong><span>, click the </span><strong><span>Options</span></strong><span> link</span></li><li><span>Check the box next to </span><em><span>Manage PayPal Disputes in the Braintree Control Panel</span></em></li><li><span>Click the </span><strong><span>Update</span></strong><span> button</span></li></ol><h2 id="dispute-stages" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Dispute stages</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#dispute-stages" aria-label="Dispute stages"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>Disputed PayPal transactions have 3 stages:</span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><a href="/braintree/articles/guides/payment-methods/paypal/disputes#retrievals"><strong><span>Retrieval</span></strong></a><span>: The customer initiates a dispute through PayPal, and you have the opportunity to resolve the
            issue with the customer directly
        </span></li><li><strong><span>Claim</span></strong><span>: The retrieval has been escalated, and PayPal will step in to investigate
            the case and determine the outcome
        </span></li><li><strong><span>Chargeback</span></strong><span>: The customer disagrees with the outcome of the claim and escalates
            the dispute to their bank; PayPal is now a facilitator, and the customer's bank will determine
            the outcome
        </span></li></ul><h2 id="timeline" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Timeline</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#timeline" aria-label="Timeline"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Once your customer initiates a retrieval, the disputed transaction amount will be placed on a
        temporary hold. If you or your customer escalate to PayPal, the retrieval will be closed out and a
        new PayPal dispute will be opened in your Braintree Control Panel.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Every dispute has a reply-by date listed in the Control Panel. At 12am on this date, you will lose
        the opportunity to take action. The time zone for your Control Panel – and this cutoff – was
        established during the Braintree application process; to view your current time zone settings,
        click on the gear icon in the top right corner, click </span><strong><span>Business</span></strong><span> from the drop-down
        menu, and scroll to the </span><strong><span>Time Zone</span></strong><span> section.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>Once an outcome has been determined, PayPal will release the funds to the winning party.</span></p><div role="alert" class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__1QhIq ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert--note__gRM6J"><div class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__header__T2Wkg"><style data-emotion="css 4757ty-svg-size_xs">.css-4757ty-svg-size_xs{speak:none;display:inline-block;aspect-ratio:1;width:1.125rem;height:1.125rem;}.css-4757ty-svg-size_xs img,.css-4757ty-svg-size_xs svg{width:100%;height:100%;display:block;}</style><span class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__icon__puACD css-4757ty-svg-size_xs" data-ppui-info="icons_8.21.2" role="img" aria-label="note" data-ppui="true"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" fill="currentColor" viewBox="0 0 24 24" width="1em" height="1em" data-ppui="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M21 12a9 9 0 1 1-18 0 9 9 0 0 1 18 0zm-9.662 5h1.32c.18 0 .276-.096.276-.276v-5.925c0-.18-.096-.276-.276-.276h-1.32c-.18 0-.276.096-.276.276v5.925c0 .18.096.276.276.276zM12 9a1 1 0 1 0 0-2 1 1 0 0 0 0 2z" clip-rule="evenodd" data-ppui="true"></path></svg></span><span class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__title__FOcbD">Note</span><span class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__content__2-Ddi"><p class="markdown-p"><span>PayPal's Seller Protection may affect the movement of money in the event of a dispute.</span></p></span></div></div><h2 id="notifications" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Notifications</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#notifications" aria-label="Notifications"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        When one of your PayPal customers initiates a dispute, you’ll receive an email with instructions
        on how to proceed. By default, the first admin
        </span><a href="/braintree/articles/control-panel/users-roles/managing-users-roles"><span>user</span></a><span> created in
        your gateway will receive notifications, but this can be adjusted in the Control Panel.
    </span></p><h3 id="updating-notification-settings" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Updating notification settings</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#updating-notification-settings" aria-label="Updating notification settings"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>To edit your notifications:</span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>Log into the </span><a href="https://www.braintreegateway.com/login"><span>Control Panel</span></a></li><li><span>Click on the gear icon in the top right corner</span></li><li><span>Click </span><strong><span>Processing</span></strong><span> from the drop-down menu</span></li><li><span>Scroll to the </span><strong><span>Notifications</span></strong><span> section</span></li><li><span>Next to </span><strong><span>Dispute Notifications</span></strong><span>, click the </span><strong><span>Options</span></strong><span> link</span></li></ol><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Here, you can adjust the frequency of the notifications and designate who receives them. Any
        merchant accounts that do not currently have a recipient designated will also be listed here;
        while not all merchant accounts will generate dispute notifications, we recommend that you assign
        notification recipients to all merchant accounts.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        When designating recipients, you have two options – adding a </span><em><span>User Recipient</span></em><span>, or using the
        </span><em><span>Email Address</span></em><span> field.
    </span></p><h4 id="user-recipients" class="test_h4 markdown-h4"><span>User Recipients</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#user-recipients" aria-label="User Recipients"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h4><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        The </span><em><span>User Recipients</span></em><span> field allows you to send notifications to existing users in the
        Control Panel. These recipients will receive notifications based on their role and any
        sub-merchants they are associated with (if applicable). If the user's permissions don’t allow them
        to see certain transactions, or they are not associated with a certain sub-merchant, they won’t
        receive dispute notifications for those transactions.
    </span></p><h4 id="email-address" class="test_h4 markdown-h4"><span>Email Address</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#email-address" aria-label="Email Address"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h4><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        The </span><em><span>Email Address</span></em><span> field allows you to send notifications to any email address, regardless
        of whether the email is associated with a user in the Control Panel. This can be particularly
        helpful if you use internal distribution lists to manage disputes (e.g.
        </span><a rel="noopener,noreferrer" href="/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#a38cc7cad0d3d6d7c6d0e3daccd6d1c0ccced3c2cdda8dc0ccce" pa-marked="1" target="_blank"><span><span class="__cf_email__" data-cfemail="395d504a494c4d5c4a7940564c4b5a565449585740175a5654">[email&#160;protected]</span></span></a><span>). Emails entered in this field will receive all dispute notifications – even if the address
        corresponds to a user with limited permissions, they will still receive notifications for all
        disputes.
    </span></p><h3 id="dispute-webhooks" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Dispute webhooks</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#dispute-webhooks" aria-label="Dispute webhooks"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        In addition to email notifications, you can set up webhooks to notify you if a dispute's
        </span><a href="/braintree/articles/guides/payment-methods/paypal/disputes#statuses"><span>status</span></a><span> is updated to </span><em><span>Open</span></em><span>, </span><em><span>Won</span></em><span>, or </span><em><span>Lost</span></em><span>. For
        more information on setting up webhooks,
        </span><a href="/braintree/docs/guides/webhooks/overview"><span>see our webhooks guide</span></a><span>.
    </span></p><h2 id="fees" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Fees</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#fees" aria-label="Fees"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        How a PayPal dispute is initiated, and whether or not it is escalated to the customer's bank or
        credit card issuer, will determine which fees you incur.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Retrievals and claims initiated and resolved through PayPal or your Braintree gateway do not incur
        dispute fees. However, you will be subject to any fees assessed by the issuer or bank account
        provider if the dispute is escalated to a chargeback or the customer initiates a claim directly
        through the issuer or bank account provider.

        your credit card disputes are not currently managed in the Control Panel, then only your PayPal

        through your normal workflow. </span><a href="/braintree/help"><span>Contact us</span></a><span> with any questions.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>

    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        If you recently received a notice that the Disputes section of your Control Panel has been
        updated, follow these steps to manage your disputes:
    </span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>Log into the </span><a href="https://www.braintreegateway.com/login"><span>Control Panel</span></a></li><li><span>Click on </span><strong><span>Disputes</span></strong><span> in the navigation bar</span></li><li><span>

                </span></li><li><strong><span>View Customer</span></strong><span>: View more information about the customer who raised this

            </span><strong><span>Claim</span></strong><span>, and </span><strong><span>Chargeback</span></strong><span>. In order to keep your Braintree dispute
            experience consistent, we label the </span><strong><span>Dispute</span></strong><span> stage as
            </span><strong><span>Retrievals</span></strong><span> in your Braintree Control Panel.
        </span></p></span></div></div><p class="markdown-p"><span>With each retrieval, you have 3 options for action:</span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Message with the customer</span></li><li><span>Send or accept an offer</span></li><li><span>Escalate to PayPal</span></li></ul><p class="markdown-p"><span>

        offers, extend counter offers, or escalate the retrieval to PayPal.

        </span></p></span></div></div><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        If a retrieval can't be resolved, either you or the customer can escalate the dispute to a claim –
        making PayPal the decider in the case. To escalate the retrieval yourself, click the
        </span><strong><span>Escalate to PayPal</span></strong><span> link in the </span><strong><span>Dispute Details</span></strong><span> section of the
        Braintree Control Panel. The original retrieval will be closed, a new dispute will be opened, and

    </span></p><h3 id="accepting" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Accepting</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#accepting" aria-label="Accepting"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        When you accept a claim or chargeback, the funds will be returned to the customer. Accepting a
        PayPal dispute indicates that you don’t wish to take any further action, but it doesn’t
        necessarily imply that you agree with the customer.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Merchants typically accept disputes if the transaction amount is minor enough that it doesn’t
        justify the work of submitting evidence, or if the transaction is known to be fraudulent.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>To accept a PayPal dispute:</span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><a href="/braintree/articles/guides/payment-methods/paypal/disputes#managing-in-the-braintree-control-panel"><span>Locate the dispute in the Control Panel</span></a></li><li><span>Click the </span><strong><span>Accept</span></strong><span> button</span></li><li><span>Confirm your acceptance by clicking the </span><strong><span>OK</span></strong><span> button</span></li></ol><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        The status of the dispute will be updated to </span><em><span>Accepted</span></em><span> in the Control Panel, and the funds
        will be returned to the customer.
    </span></p><h3 id="disputing" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Disputing</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#disputing" aria-label="Disputing"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>If you believe the transaction was legitimate, you can dispute the claim or chargeback:</span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><a href="/braintree/articles/guides/payment-methods/paypal/disputes#managing-in-the-braintree-control-panel"><span>Locate the dispute in the Control Panel</span></a></li><li><span>

            the </span><strong><span>Dispute</span></strong><span> button
        </span></li><li><span>Complete the </span><a href="/braintree/articles/guides/payment-methods/paypal/disputes#uploading-evidence"><span>evidence requirements</span></a></li><li><span>Click the </span><strong><span>Submit Dispute</span></strong><span> button</span></li></ol><h4 id="uploading-evidence" class="test_h4 markdown-h4"><span>Uploading evidence</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#uploading-evidence" aria-label="Uploading evidence"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h4><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Every dispute is different, so the evidence you provide will vary depending on the nature of the
        dispute and the documents you have available. The more thorough your evidence is, the better.
        Evidence includes, but is not limited to:
    </span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>
            Tracking information from a shipment, particularly if a signature was obtained at the time of
            delivery
        </span></li><li><span>
            Documentation of the customer's agreement to terms of service that includes both the customer's
            name and email address
        </span></li><li><span>Emails or other correspondence with the customer that support your case</span></li><li><span>Evidence of refund or replacement of goods already sent to the customer</span></li></ul><h2 id="statuses" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Statuses</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#statuses" aria-label="Statuses"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        PayPal disputes in the Braintree Control Panel have the same statuses as your credit card
        disputes:
    </span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><em><span>Open</span></em><span>: The claim or chargeback has been issued and we are awaiting your response</span></li><li><em><span>Accepted</span></em><span>: You have opted out of providing evidence for the case and the funds will be
            returned to the customer
        </span></li><li><em><span>Auto Accepted</span></em><span>: The dispute has been automatically accepted for the best merchant
            experience
        </span></li><li><em><span>Disputed</span></em><span>: Your case is under review and no action is needed at this time</span></li><li><em><span>Won</span></em><span>: The case has been ruled in your favor and the funds will be released back to you
        </span></li><li><em><span>Lost</span></em><span>: The case has been ruled in the customer's favor and the funds will be returned to
            them
        </span></li><li><em><span>Expired</span></em><span>: The reply-by date to submit evidence has passed and you have forfeited your
            right to dispute the case – the funds will be returned back to the customer
        </span></li></ul><h3 id="a-note-on-disputed-statuses" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>A note on Disputed statuses</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#a-note-on-disputed-statuses" aria-label="A note on Disputed statuses"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>For Braintree, cases with the </span><em><span>Disputed</span></em><span> status can mean one of two things:</span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Your evidence has been submitted and the case is being evaluated</span></li><li><span>The case is currently </span><em><span>Under Review</span></em><span> with PayPal with no evidence required</span></li></ul><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Cases listed as </span><em><span>Under Review</span></em><span> in the PayPal Resolution Center have not yet had evidence
        submitted, but show as </span><em><span>Disputed</span></em><span> in the Braintree Control Panel. In cases where PayPal
        deems that evidence is required, the case may change from </span><em><span>Disputed</span></em><span> back to </span><em><span>Open</span></em><span>.
        At that point, you would need to submit evidence and </span><a href="/braintree/articles/guides/payment-methods/paypal/disputes#disputing"><span>dispute</span></a><span> the case.

At 12am on this date, you will lose the opportunity to take action. The time zone for your Control Panel – and this cutoff – was established during the Braintree application process; to view your current time zone settings, click on the gear icon in the top right corner, click **Business** from the drop-down menu, and scroll to the **Time Zone** section.\n\nOnce an outcome has been determined, PayPal will release the funds to the winning party.\n\n\n**NOTE**\n PayPal's Seller Protection may affect the movement of money in the event of a dispute.\n\n \n\n\n## Notifications\n\nWhen one of your PayPal customers initiates a dispute, you’ll receive an email with instructions on how to proceed.
