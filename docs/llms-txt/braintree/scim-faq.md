href="https://developer.paypal.com/braintree/articles/control-panel/users-roles/scim/scim-integration" target="_blank"><span>here</span></a><span>.&nbsp;</span></p><h2 id="what-is-scim" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>What is SCIM?</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#what-is-scim" aria-label="What is SCIM?"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    SCIM, or the
    System for Cross-domain Identity Management specification, is an open standard for managing user identity information. SCIM provides
    a defined schema for representing users and groups and a RESTful API to run
    CRUD operations on those user and group resources. Essentially, SCIM helps
    you (the merchant) to use the IdP (Identity Providers) of your choice to
    manage your users across multiple integrations, whether BT or a different
    company.
  </span></p><h2 id="why-scim" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Why SCIM?</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#why-scim" aria-label="Why SCIM?"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    We have many merchants. You have many users — hundreds, if not thousands —
    all of whom need to be onboarded, offboarded, modified, or suspended. You'll
    need to do this quickly, easily, and in as few operations as possible. Doing
    this across multiple integrations (such as BT + others) is a pain. So why
    not do it all in one place? With SCIM, merchants (for example, you) will be
    able to fully control the provisioning and de-provisioning of users and
    groups (roles, merchant accounts, grant all merchant accounts) from your own
    IdPs (Okta, OneLogin, Entra, and so on). This is a huge boon for larger
    merchants who have a massive amount of users interacting with the Braintree
    system. With SCIM, instead of removing users one by one from a role, you can
    now remove all of them from the role with one operation. Likewise, you can
    onboard users in bulk, offboard users in bulk, and modify permissions in
    bulk. It seems much simpler, doesn't it?
  </span></p><h2 id="how-does-scim-work" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>How does SCIM work?</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#how-does-scim-work" aria-label="How does SCIM work?"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    Essentially, when we set up SCIM for you, we do some data setup on our side,
    and then we give you an access token for your IDP to interact with us.
    Consider this similar to an API key or password that helps their service
    make requests that modify your users or groups on your behalf. After you
    give that access token to your IdP of choice, we sync up our user data. So,
    your IDP of choice has the same user data/state as we do. After that's in
    place, any time you want to make a change to your users, you just make that
    change in your IdP, and then your IdP will send us the necessary requests to
    keep our data and their data in sync. For example, if you remove a few users
    from being able to access a merchant account, your IdP will send us a
    request to remove those users' access to that merchant account. This way, we
    keep in sync and everyone's happy. The best part about all this is that we
    and the IdPs all use an industry-standard protocol, so we all stay on the
    same page. If you're interested, you can read more about it on SCIM's RFC page, RFC 7644.&nbsp;Something to note with this is that after you onboard to SCIM with us, you
    can't go off. It's a one-way street. In addition, after you're on SCIM, you
    won't be able to edit/create/delete SSO users on the BT control panel. This
    is to keep us and your IdP in sync. For your non-SSO users, they will stay
    non-SSO and will still be managed using the BT control panel (and only the
    BT control panel). Only SSO users can be SCIM users.</span></p><h2 id="troubleshooting-and-faqs" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Troubleshooting and FAQs</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#troubleshooting-and-faqs" aria-label="Troubleshooting and FAQs"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"></p><h5 id="what-if-there-s-a-problem-with-my-scim-integration-and-i-want-to-roll-off--once-rolled-off--what-does-rolling-back-onto-scim-look-like" class="test_h5 markdown-h5"><span>What if there's a problem with my SCIM integration and I want to roll off? Once rolled off, what does rolling back onto SCIM look like?</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#what-if-there-s-a-problem-with-my-scim-integration-and-i-want-to-roll-off--once-rolled-off--what-does-rolling-back-onto-scim-look-like" aria-label="What if there&#x27;s a problem with my SCIM integration and I want to roll off? Once rolled off, what does rolling back onto SCIM look like?"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h5><p class="markdown-p"><span>SCIM acts essentially as a mirror between our data and the IdP's data. Changes done on one side, must be propogated to the other seamlessly, or else one of us gets out of sync, and its gets very messy to fix. Once you're onboarded to SCIM with us, this syncronization dance is something we handle for you, and handle very well. However, if you're not satisfied with our SCIM product, or something goes wrong, we can roll you off of SCIM. However, once we roll you off, it can be somewhat messy to roll you back onto SCIM later. This is due to the syncronization dance we have to perform when it comes to SCIM and keeping our side and the IdP together. Say we roll you off. Then, you make changes on the IdP side. Because SCIM is turned off, these changes are not propogated to us automatically. In addition, you make some more changes on our side. Again, because SCIM is disabled, these changes are not propogated to your IdP. Now, when we try to turn SCIM back on for you, both sides have changes the other side does not contain. Now, we have some steps put in place during our re-enablement flow that should generally resolve these mismatches in most cases. However, in extreme cases (we're talking thousands of users and hundreds of changes across many groups), it can be a bit messy to get the IdP re-synced with our side.&nbsp;</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><br/></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>All this to say, is that we want to avoid rolling you off SCIM whenever possible. SCIM provides a defined schema for representing users and groups and a RESTful API to run CRUD operations on those user and group resources. Essentially, SCIM helps you (the merchant) to use the IdP (Identity Providers) of your choice to manage your users across multiple integrations, whether BT or a different company.\n\n\n## Why SCIM?\n\nWe have many merchants. You have many users — hundreds, if not thousands — all of whom need to be onboarded, offboarded, modified, or suspended. You'll need to do this quickly, easily, and in as few operations as possible. Doing this across multiple integrations (such as BT + others) is a pain. So why not do it all in one place? With SCIM, merchants (for example, you) will be able to fully control the provisioning and de-provisioning of users and groups (roles, merchant accounts, grant all merchant accounts) from your own IdPs (Okta, OneLogin, Entra, and so on). This is a huge boon for larger merchants who have a massive amount of users interacting with the Braintree system. With SCIM, instead of removing users one by one from a role, you can now remove all of them from the role with one operation. Likewise, you can onboard users in bulk, offboard users in bulk, and modify permissions in bulk. It seems much simpler, doesn't it?\n\n\n## How does SCIM work?\n\nEssentially, when we set up SCIM for you, we do some data setup on our side, and then we give you an access token for your IDP to interact with us. Consider this similar to an API key or password that helps their service make requests that modify your users or groups on your behalf. After you give that access token to your IdP of choice, we sync up our user data. So, your IDP of choice has the same user data/state as we do. After that's in place, any time you want to make a change to your users, you just make that change in your IdP, and then your IdP will send us the necessary requests to keep our data and their data in sync. For example, if you remove a few users from being able to access a merchant account, your IdP will send us a request to remove those users' access to that merchant account. This way, we keep in sync and everyone's happy. The best part about all this is that we and the IdPs all use an industry-standard protocol, so we all stay on the same page. If you're interested, you can read more about it on SCIM's RFC page, RFC 7644.Something to note with this is that after you onboard to SCIM with us, you can't go off. It's a one-way street. In addition, after you're on SCIM, you won't be able to edit/create/delete SSO users on the BT control panel. This is to keep us and your IdP in sync. For your non-SSO users, they will stay non-SSO and will still be managed using the BT control panel (and only the BT control panel). Only SSO users can be SCIM users.\n\n\n## Troubleshooting and FAQs\n\n\n\n\n##### What if there's a problem with my SCIM integration and I want to roll off? Once rolled off, what does rolling back onto SCIM look like?\n\nSCIM acts essentially as a mirror between our data and the IdP's data. Changes done on one side, must be propogated to the other seamlessly, or else one of us gets out of sync, and its gets very messy to fix. Once you're onboarded to SCIM with us, this syncronization dance is something we handle for you, and handle very well. However, if you're not satisfied with our SCIM product, or something goes wrong, we can roll you off of SCIM. However, once we roll you off, it can be somewhat messy to roll you back onto SCIM later. This is due to the syncronization dance we have to perform when it comes to SCIM and keeping our side and the IdP together. Say we roll you off. Then, you make changes on the IdP side. Because SCIM is turned off, these changes are not propogated to us automatically. In addition, you make some more changes on our side. Again, because SCIM is disabled, these changes are not propogated to your IdP. Now, when we try to turn SCIM back on for you, both sides have changes the other side does not contain. Now, we have some steps put in place during our re-enablement flow that should generally resolve these mismatches in most cases. However, in extreme cases (we're talking thousands of users and hundreds of changes across many groups), it can be a bit messy to get the IdP re-synced with our side.\n\n\n\nAll this to say, is that we want to avoid rolling you off SCIM whenever possible. We can do it, but then getting back onto SCIM requires additional caution and patience, with which we can and do build into our re-enablement processes.\n\n\n##### Now that I'm on SCIM, I'm not able to create new SSO users or edit existing ones.
