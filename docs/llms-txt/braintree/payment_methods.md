class="main-article-content"><div class="ecmRichText" style="margin-bottom:0px"><h1><span>Payment Methods</span></h1><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    A
    </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/reference/#Object--PaymentMethod"><code><span>PaymentMethod</span></code></a><span>
    represents transactable payment information such as credit card details or a customer's
    authorization to charge a PayPal or Venmo account. Payment methods belong to a
    </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/guides/customers"><code><span>customer</span></code></a><span>, are securely stored in the Braintree Vault, and have an </span><code><span>id</span></code><span> attribute that you can
    store on your servers (with reduced PCI compliance burden) and later use to create
    </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/guides/transactions/#creating-transactions"><code><span>create transactions</span></code></a><span>.
</span></p><h2 id="obtain-a-single-use-payment-method--nonce" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Obtain A Single-Use Payment Method (Nonce)</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#obtain-a-single-use-payment-method--nonce" aria-label="Obtain A Single-Use Payment Method (Nonce)"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    Follow
    </span><a href="/braintree/docs/start/hello-server#receive-a-payment-method-nonce-from-your-client"><span>this guide</span></a><span>
    to obtain a payment method nonce from your client.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    In this context, you can think of the payment method nonce string as a unique ID for a credit
    card, PayPal account, or any other payment method type that you've already sent to Braintree from
    your client-side integration. In the GraphQL API, this is represented by the
    </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/reference/#Object--PaymentMethod"><code><span>PaymentMethod</span></code></a><span> type,
    with its </span><code><span>usage</span></code><span> field containing a value of </span><code><span>SINGLE_USE</span></code><span>. The ID for this
    tokenized, single-use payment method information is the only thing you need to vault it.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    For the remainder of this guide, we will refer to the payment method represented by the nonce as a
    "single-use payment method", and the nonce itself as its ID.
</span></p><h2 id="vaulting-the-payment-method" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Vaulting The Payment Method</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#vaulting-the-payment-method" aria-label="Vaulting The Payment Method"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    Once you have a single-use payment method ID from your client, you can vault the underlying
    payment method. To do so, use the
    </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/reference/#Mutation--vaultPaymentMethod"><code><span>vaultPaymentMethod</span></code></a><span>
    mutation.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    This mutation will automatically run a verification against payment methods that support them
    before storing them in the vault. The verification result is represented both at the top level as
    </span><code><span>vaultPaymentMethod.verification</span></code><span>, and on the payment method itself in
    </span><code><span>vaultPaymentMethod.paymentMethod.verifications</span></code><span>.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    Note that the single-use payment method will be consumed if it is successfully vaulted. You will
    not be able to use it again. Instead, the mutation will return a new, multi-use payment method
    representing the same payment information. Use the ID returned by the

    If you want to group payment methods together in the Braintree API by individual customer, you can
    pass a </span><code><span>customerId</span></code><span> in the vault request. Once vaulted, a payment method cannot be
    transferred to a different customer, but the customer can be updated with new personal
    information. All payment methods belonging to a customer will be available on the
    </span><code><span>paymentMethods</span></code><span> connection field on the
    </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/reference/#Object--Customer"><code><span>Customer</span></code></a><span>.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    The default behavior when vaulting a payment method, without a </span><code><span>customerId</span></code><span>, is to
    create an empty customer associated with the newly-vaulted payment method. This can be ignored if
    unused, or updated later to hold relevant information.
</span></p><h3 id="error-handling" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Error Handling</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#error-handling" aria-label="Error Handling"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    The only required parameter on input is </span><code><span>paymentMethodId</span></code><span>. Failing to supply this will
    result in an error.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>If the vaulting mutation fails otherwise, it is most likely because:</span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>
        the </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/guides/payment_methods/#payment-method-verification-failed"><span>payment method verification failed</span></a><span> (for
        payment methods that require it)
    </span></li><li><span>
        the
        </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/guides/payment_methods/#single-use-payment-method-is-consumed"><span>single-use payment method has already been consumed</span></a></li><li><span>
        the </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/guides/payment_methods/#single-use-payment-method-has-expired"><span>single-use payment method has expired</span></a></li><li><span>
        the
        </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/guides/payment_methods/#single-use-payment-method-represents-invalid-data"><span>single-use payment method represents invalid payment information</span></a></li><li><span>
        the
        </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/guides/payment_methods/#single-use-payment-method-isnt-vaultable"><span>single-use payment method represents payment information that isn't vaultable</span></a></li></ul><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    Depending on which error you receive, you will need to prompt your customer to re-enter or enter
    another payment method.

</span></p><h4 id="single-use-payment-method-has-expired" class="test_h4 markdown-h4"><span>Single-Use Payment Method has Expired</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#single-use-payment-method-has-expired" aria-label="Single-Use Payment Method has Expired"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h4><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    Single-use payment methods expire after 3 hours. If it has been more than 3 hours since you
    </span><a href="/braintree/graphql/guides/collecting_payment_information"><span>collected payment information</span></a><span>
    to create a single-use payment method, you can no longer consume it.
</span></p><h4 id="single-use-payment-method-represents-invalid-data" class="test_h4 markdown-h4"><span>Single-Use Payment Method Represents Invalid Data</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#single-use-payment-method-represents-invalid-data" aria-label="Single-Use Payment Method Represents Invalid Data"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h4><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    When the payment information a single-use payment method represents is "not valid," the vaulting
    mutation will return a validation error.
</span></p><h4 id="single-use-payment-method-isn-t-vaultable" class="test_h4 markdown-h4"><span>Single-Use Payment Method Isn't Vaultable</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#single-use-payment-method-isn-t-vaultable" aria-label="Single-Use Payment Method Isn&#x27;t Vaultable"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h4><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    In some cases, the single-use payment method represents payment information that is not vaultable.
    For example, when authorizing a PayPal account, the customer only grants permission to charge it
    once. In that case, you are not permitted to retain the information in your vault. Any calls to
    the vaulting mutation with such a single-use payment method will result in an error.
