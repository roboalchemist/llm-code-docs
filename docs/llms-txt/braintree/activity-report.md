  The Activity Report is a daily report that contains all transactions that were
  </span><em><span>Submitted for Settlement</span></em><span> or </span><em><span>Settlement Declined</span></em><span> from the previous processing
  day. The purpose of this report is to provide projected disbursement dates and total transaction
  fees for all transactions to aid in forecasting receivables from PayPal. By saving the projected
  disbursement dates for all transactions in your system of record, you will be able to more easily
  track transactions that will be disbursed in the future. For merchants who offer installments,
  this report will also capture any adjustments to future installment disbursement that may occur
  due to credit card disputes or refunds.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  For merchants who process credit cards (with or without installments), this report will contain
  the </span><em><span>Projected Disbursement Date</span></em><span> for the following type of transactions:
</span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Credit card sale and refund transactions</span></li><li><span>Debit card sale and refund transactions</span></li><li><span>Newly created installment transactions</span></li><li><span>Adjustments to installments</span></li></ul><h2 id="obtaining-projected-disbursement-dates" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Obtaining projected disbursement dates</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#obtaining-projected-disbursement-dates" aria-label="Obtaining projected disbursement dates"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  In Brazil, card-based transactions can take anywhere from 2 to 30 days to be disbursed depending
  on the card type. The default disbursement is 30 days, however a feature called Predefined Funds
  Anticipation allows merchants to pay a premium to accelerate the funding of credit card based
  transactions. For more information about this feature
  </span><a href="/braintree/articles/br/reporting-reconciliation/transaction-level-fee-report#funds-anticipation"><span>visit this page</span></a><span>.

  Whether you opt to pay for Predefined Funds Anticipation or not, it will be important to obtain
  and store the projected disbursement dates for all transactions and installments in your system of
  record so that you can track your receivables and forecast future disbursements.
</span></p><h3 id="standard-sale-and-refund-transactions" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Standard sale and refund transactions</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#standard-sale-and-refund-transactions" aria-label="Standard sale and refund transactions"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  To obtain the projected disbursement date for a standard sale or refund transaction, follow the
  below steps:
</span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>
    Filter the report on </span><em><span>Record Type</span></em><span> = </span><strong><span>Sale</span></strong><span> or </span><strong><span>Refund</span></strong></li><li><span>
    Use the values in found in the </span><em><span>Record ID</span></em><span> column to match the IDs of transaction sales
    and refunds in your system of records
  </span></li><li><span>
    Save the value found in the </span><em><span>Projected Disbursement Data</span></em><span> column along with the
    transaction in your system
  </span></li></ol><h3 id="sale-transactions-with-installments" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Sale transactions with installments</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#sale-transactions-with-installments" aria-label="Sale transactions with installments"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  When a sale transaction is created and submitted for settlement with installments, the transaction
  response object will contain the following data for each installment:
</span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Installment ID</span></li><li><span>Installment Amount</span></li></ul><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  When the installments are initially created, the </span><em><span>Projected Disbursement Date</span></em><span> will be
  </span><em><span>NULL</span></em><span> and will be populated within twelve hours.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  Installment records should be stored separately from standard sale transactions in your system of
  record because they will be paid out over time, so tracking them individually will be required in
  order to reconcile PayPal’s data with your system of record, and with your deposit bank account.
</span></p><h4 id="steps-to-locate-installment-projected-disbursement-dates" class="test_h4 markdown-h4"><span>Steps to locate installment projected disbursement dates</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#steps-to-locate-installment-projected-disbursement-dates" aria-label="Steps to locate installment projected disbursement dates"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h4><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>
    Select all records where </span><em><span>Record Type</span></em><span> = </span><em><span>Installment</span></em><span> and
    </span><em><span>Record Subtype</span></em><span> is empty.
  </span></li><li><span>
    Once the installment records are filtered to just those that represent newly created
    installments, iterate through the set using the </span><strong><span>Record ID</span></strong><span> column to identify
    the installment ID for which the record pertains to.
  </span></li><li><span>
    Locate the </span><strong><span>Projected Disbursement Date</span></strong><span> field, add this date to your system of
    record, and save it with the installment record.
  </span></li></ol><h2 id="installment-adjustments" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Installment adjustments</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#installment-adjustments" aria-label="Installment adjustments"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>The amount of an installment can be affected by two types of payment events:</span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Full and partial refunds</span></li><li><span>Full and partial disputes</span></li></ul><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  Whenever a transaction with installments is refunded or has a dispute that has
  </span><em><span>Lost</span></em><span> associated with it, every installment will have its amount adjusted equally.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  Additionally, adjustments can occur on both installments previously disbursed as well as
  installments that are to be disbursed in the future.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  In the case an adjustment is made to an installment that has previously been disbursed, the
  adjustment amount will be netted out of the next disbursement, so it’s important to track the
  installment adjustments as they are created so that you can reconcile the installment
  disbursements with both your deposit account and your system of record.
</span></p><h4 id="types-of-installment-adjustments" class="test_h4 markdown-h4"><span>Types of installment adjustments</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#types-of-installment-adjustments" aria-label="Types of installment adjustments"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h4><h5 id="refunds" class="test_h5 markdown-h5"><span>Refunds</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#refunds" aria-label="Refunds"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h5><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  In the case of refunds, each installment will be reduced by the amount of the refund divided by
  the number of installments.
</span></p><h5 id="disputes" class="test_h5 markdown-h5"><span>Disputes</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#disputes" aria-label="Disputes"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h5><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  Dispute adjustments are typically negative adjustments and will reduce installment amounts. In
  some cases, disputes can be reversed, resulting in a positive adjustment.
</span></p><h4 id="steps-to-locate-information-in-the-activity-report" class="test_h4 markdown-h4"><span>Steps to locate information in the Activity Report</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#steps-to-locate-information-in-the-activity-report" aria-label="Steps to locate information in the Activity Report"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h4><p class="markdown-p"><span>

  adjustment records separately.
</span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>

    </span><em><span>Installment</span></em><span> and </span><em><span>Record Subtype</span></em><span> = </span><em><span>Adjustment</span></em><span> from the Daily Activity
    Report
  </span></li><li><span>

    </span><ul class="list list--disc"><li><em><span>Settlement Amount</span></em><span> – This will be used to obtain the amount of the adjustment. Add

        the installment.
      </span></li><li><em><span>Installment Disbursed</span></em><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>

          </span></li><li><span>

          </span></li></ul></li><li><em><span>Projected Disbursement Date</span></em><span> – This is the date the adjustment amount will be netted
        from a future disbursement
      </span></li><li><em><span>Reason</span></em><span> – This is the reason for the adjustment. The reason will either be a
        </span><strong><span>refund</span></strong><span> or </span><strong><span>dispute</span></strong><span>.
      </span></li><li><em><span>Adjuster ID</span></em><span> – This is the ID of the object in your system that caused the

        </span><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>

          </span></li><li><span>
