    ACH Direct Debit is a payment method that runs on the ACH (Automated Clearing House) network in

    bank account, as opposed to processing through a card brand.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>At a high level, accepting ACH payments consists of four concepts:</span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Tokenizing</span></li><li><span>Vaulting</span></li><li><span>Verifying</span></li><li><span>Transacting</span></li></ul><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    Depending on how you wish to verify your customer's bank account, these concepts will overlap in
    different ways.
</span></p><h2 id="tokenizing" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Tokenizing</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#tokenizing" aria-label="Tokenizing"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
    Tokenizing is the process of exchanging raw payment information for a secure, single-use payment
    method ID.
</span></p><h3 id="tokenizing-without-verification" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Tokenizing without verification</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#tokenizing-without-verification" aria-label="Tokenizing without verification"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
