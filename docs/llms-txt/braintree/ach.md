class="main-article-content"><div class="ecmRichText" style="margin-bottom:0px"><section><h1><span>ACH Direct Debit</span></h1><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        ACH, or Automated Clearing House, is a payment method that runs on banking networks in the United
        States. It allows customers to pay for transactions by debiting directly from their bank account,
        as opposed to processing through a card brand.
    </span></p><h2 id="availability" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Availability</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#availability" aria-label="Availability"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>ACH Direct Debit is available for merchants who:</span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Are located in the United States</span></li><li><span>Transact in the US in USD</span></li><li><span>Are processing with Braintree Direct</span></li><li><span>Are in good standing (i.e. not on a chargeback program)</span></li><li><span>
            Can implement a custom client-side integration using our
            </span><a href="/braintree/docs/guides/client-sdk/setup/javascript/v3"><span>JavaScript v3 SDK</span></a><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>ACH is currently not available in our Drop-in UI</span></li></ul></li></ul><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        If you meet the criteria, </span><a href="/braintree/help?issue=acceptPaymentTypes"><span>contact us</span></a><span> to
        enable ACH Direct Debit in your sandbox account.
    </span></p><h2 id="verification-methods" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Verification methods</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#verification-methods" aria-label="Verification methods"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        For ACH Direct Debit, you're required to verify a bank account before collecting money from it.
        Verifications provide a way for you to check the validity of a bank account before attempting to
        process a transaction – helping you protect yourself against fraudulent or erroneous transactions.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Braintree offers 3 different verification methods. You can use just one, but we recommend
        implementing at least one backup method in case the first method fails.
    </span></p><h3 id="network-check" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Network check</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#network-check" aria-label="Network check"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>Network check instantly verifies your customer's bank account by requiring entry of specific account information (such as routing number and account number) at checkout. While network check provides near-instantaneous verification, it is not supported by all banks.

        This method supports two types of verification:
    </span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><strong><span>Account Validation:</span></strong><span>confirms the existence of the account, i.e. is the account open and valid
        </span></li><li><strong><span>Customer Validation:</span></strong><span>verifies that your customer is the account owner. 
        </span></li></ul><span>
    Additional fees may apply to use both Account Validation and Customer Validation, depending on your pricing model. If you’re unsure which pricing model you’re on, </span><a href="https://developer.paypal.com/braintree/help/feeHelp" target="_blank"><span>contact us</span></a><span> for fee information.
    </span><p class="markdown-p"></p><h3 id="micro-transfers" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Micro-transfers</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#micro-transfers" aria-label="Micro-transfers"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Micro-transfers require your customer to first enter their bank account information (e.g. routing
        number, account number), and then confirm the amounts of two deposits we send to their provided
        account in a custom UI built by your developers. This verification process typically takes 2-3
        business days and requires two separate actions from the customer.
    </span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        While micro-transfers require additional developer work to build a UI for collecting the deposit
        amounts from your customers, they provide the most accurate bank verification method. To stay
        compliant with
        </span><a class="is-external-link dx-external-href" rel="noopener,noreferrer" href="https://www.nacha.org/" pa-marked="1" target="_blank"><span>Nacha</span></a><span>
        standards, we monitor forward and return volumes of all micro-transfers and set risk thresholds to
        alert you of any unusual activity. To reduce potential fraud risks such as spam, malware and
        botnet attacks, you should consider implementing advanced fraud prevention and anomaly detection
        solutions when building the UI.
    </span></p><h3 id="independent-check" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Independent check</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#independent-check" aria-label="Independent check"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        If you'd like to manually manage verification, you can do so with independent check. There are two
        options:
    </span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Collect physically voided checks to verify the account</span></li><li><span>Use another third-party verification provider</span></li></ul><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        While this verification option gives you the most flexibility, it can also be the riskiest. If
        your independent method doesn't accurately verify accounts, it may negatively impact your
        transaction approval rates in the long run.
    </span></p><h3 id="business-versus-personal-bank-accounts" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Business versus personal bank accounts</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#business-versus-personal-bank-accounts" aria-label="Business versus personal bank accounts"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        If you choose to verify bank accounts using the network check, micro-transfers, or independent
        check methods, one of the fields we require you to collect is the </span><em><span>account ownership type</span></em><span>,
        which can be either business or personal. While you can process transactions on both types of
        accounts, it's important to record the type for each account accurately. If you submit a
        transaction with the wrong account ownership type, the bank may decline the transaction request;
        this is commonly referred to as a return.
    </span></p><h3 id="paypal-company-id-and-company-name" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>PayPal Company ID and Company Name</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#paypal-company-id-and-company-name" aria-label="PayPal Company ID and Company Name"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Another reason for a return may be that your customer has a debit block in place. If a transaction
        is returned for this reason, you'll be charged a return fee and will need to ask your customer to
        call their bank to get the debit block lifted from their bank account. Since ACH Direct Debit
        transactions are submitted to the ACH Network with the PayPal Company Name and Company ID in the
        NACHA file, they will need to provide their bank with the following Company IDs:
    </span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>PAYPALMTCU</span></li><li><span>PAYPALMTBU</span></li></ul><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        In addition, your customers may see "PAYPAL," "PAYPALMTCU," or "PAYPALMTBU" in the transaction
        descriptor in their statements.
    </span></p><h2 id="processing" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Processing</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#processing" aria-label="Processing"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        ACH is a batch-based system, meaning that transactions do not take place in real-time, and
        authorizations or captures do not exist as they do with credit card transactions. This makes it
        more difficult to verify whether funds will be available in the account at the time the
        transaction takes place. As a result, debit entries can be returned by the customer's bank for
        insufficient funds or a variety of other reasons. Because of this, the settlement and funding
        timeline is different from standard credit cards.
    </span></p><h3 id="settlement-and-funding-timeline" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Settlement and funding timeline</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#settlement-and-funding-timeline" aria-label="Settlement and funding timeline"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
        Before we disburse funds to your account, we wait for 3 business days for any returns that the
        customer's bank sends through the ACH network. If we are notified in that timeframe that the
        transaction has been returned and can't be processed, the transaction is declined and the funds
        will not be disbursed. It will be up to you to follow-up with your customer for payment. If the
        transaction is not returned to us in 3 business days, we assume the transaction is valid and will
        disburse funds to your bank account which takes 2 additional business days, totaling 5 business
        days. Returns can be received after the 3 business day timeframe; in those cases, we deduct the
        amount from the next business day's disbursement.
    </span></p><div role="alert" class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__1QhIq ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert--note__gRM6J"><div class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__header__T2Wkg"><style data-emotion="css 4757ty-svg-size_xs">.css-4757ty-svg-size_xs{speak:none;display:inline-block;aspect-ratio:1;width:1.125rem;height:1.125rem;}.css-4757ty-svg-size_xs img,.css-4757ty-svg-size_xs svg{width:100%;height:100%;display:block;}</style><span class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__icon__puACD css-4757ty-svg-size_xs" data-ppui-info="icons_8.21.2" role="img" aria-label="note" data-ppui="true"><svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" fill="currentColor" viewBox="0 0 24 24" width="1em" height="1em" data-ppui="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M21 12a9 9 0 1 1-18 0 9 9 0 0 1 18 0zm-9.662 5h1.32c.18 0 .276-.096.276-.276v-5.925c0-.18-.096-.276-.276-.276h-1.32c-.18 0-.276.096-.276.276v5.925c0 .18.096.276.276.276zM12 9a1 1 0 1 0 0-2 1 1 0 0 0 0 2z" clip-rule="evenodd" data-ppui="true"></path></svg></span><span class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__title__FOcbD">Note</span><span class="ContextualAlert-module_pp-alert__content__2-Ddi"><p class="markdown-p"><span>
            We will disburse ACH funds together with your credit card transactions – you will only see one
            deposit that includes both credit card and ACH funds.
        </span></p></span></div></div><h3 id="transaction-statuses" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Transaction statuses</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#transaction-statuses" aria-label="Transaction statuses"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><ul class="list list--disc"><li><strong><span>Settlement Pending</span></strong><span>: The transaction was successfully submitted to the banking
            network
        </span></li><li><strong><span>Failed</span></strong><span>: An error occurred when submitting the transaction</span></li><li><strong><span>Settlement Declined</span></strong><span>: The transaction was returned by the banking network in the
            3 business days timeframe and the funds will not be disbursed to you – a $5 USD fee will apply
        </span></li><li><strong><span>Settled</span></strong><span>: The transaction was not returned in the 3 business day timeframe and
            the funds will be disbursed to you. In the event that we receive a return after a transaction
            has "settled," the status will remain "settled"
        </span></li><li><strong><span>Payment Method Blocked</span></strong><span>: The payment method was blocked because there is a
            pending Micro-transfer or the verification process failed during Micro-transfer
        </span></li></ul><p class="markdown-p"><span>Here's a more detailed timeline for what the ACH transaction lifecycle looks like:</span></p><h5 id="day-of-transaction" class="test_h5 markdown-h5"><span>Day of transaction</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#day-of-transaction" aria-label="Day of transaction"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h5><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>
            The transaction is processed and is immediately given the </span><em><span>Settlement Pending</span></em><span> status
        </span></li></ul><h5 id="2-business-days" class="test_h5 markdown-h5"><span>1-2 business days</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#2-business-days" aria-label="1-2 business days"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h5><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>

        </span></li><li><span>

            </span><em><span>Settlement Declined</span></em><span> status, and you will be notified
        </span></li></ul><h5 id="rd-business-day" class="test_h5 markdown-h5"><span>3rd business day</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#rd-business-day" aria-label="3rd business day"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h5><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>

    </span></p><div data-pm-slice="2,4,[]" data-title="Add,“Bank,Account,Blocking”,as,a,sub,section,under,the,returns,section,with,the,below,text" data-node-type="expand" data-prosemirror-content-type="node" data-expanded="true" data-prosemirror-node-block="true" data-prosemirror-node-name="expand"><h3 id="blocked-bank-accounts" class="test_h3 markdown-h3" data-prosemirror-content-type="node" data-prosemirror-node-block="true" data-prosemirror-node-name="heading"><span>Blocked Bank Accounts</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#blocked-bank-accounts" aria-label="Blocked Bank Accounts"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>To comply with NACHA rules and minimize fraud, return fees, and other risks, we block bank accounts that receive certain ACH return codes. It allows customers to pay for transactions by debiting directly from their bank account, as opposed to processing through a card brand.\n\n\n## Availability\n\nACH Direct Debit is available for merchants who:\n\n\n- Are located in the United States\n- Transact in the US in USD\n- Are processing with Braintree Direct\n- Are in good standing (i.e. not on a chargeback program)\n- Can implement a custom client-side integration using our[JavaScript v3 SDK](/braintree/docs/guides/client-sdk/setup/javascript/v3)\n- ACH is currently not available in our Drop-in UI\n\n\n\nIf you meet the criteria, [contact us](/braintree/help?issue=acceptPaymentTypes) to enable ACH Direct Debit in your sandbox account.\n\n\n## Verification methods\n\nFor ACH Direct Debit, you're required to verify a bank account before collecting money from it. Verifications provide a way for you to check the validity of a bank account before attempting to process a transaction – helping you protect yourself against fraudulent or erroneous transactions.\n\nBraintree offers 3 different verification methods. You can use just one, but we recommend implementing at least one backup method in case the first method fails.\n\n\n### Network check\n\nNetwork check instantly verifies your customer's bank account by requiring entry of specific account information (such as routing number and account number) at checkout. While network check provides near-instantaneous verification, it is not supported by all banks. This method supports two types of verification:\n\n\n- **Account Validation:**confirms the existence of the account, i.e. is the account open and valid\n- **Customer Validation:**verifies that your customer is the account owner.\n\nAdditional fees may apply to use both Account Validation and Customer Validation, depending on your pricing model. If you’re unsure which pricing model you’re on,[contact us](https://developer.paypal.com/braintree/help/feeHelp)for fee information.\n\n\n### Micro-transfers\n\nMicro-transfers require your customer to first enter their bank account information (e.g. routing number, account number), and then confirm the amounts of two deposits we send to their provided account in a custom UI built by your developers. This verification process typically takes 2-3 business days and requires two separate actions from the customer.\n\nWhile micro-transfers require additional developer work to build a UI for collecting the deposit amounts from your customers, they provide the most accurate bank verification method. To stay compliant with [Nacha](https://www.nacha.org/) standards, we monitor forward and return volumes of all micro-transfers and set risk thresholds to alert you of any unusual activity. To reduce potential fraud risks such as spam, malware and botnet attacks, you should consider implementing advanced fraud prevention and anomaly detection solutions when building the UI.\n\n\n### Independent check\n\nIf you'd like to manually manage verification, you can do so with independent check. There are two options:\n\n\n- Collect physically voided checks to verify the account\n- Use another third-party verification provider\n\nWhile this verification option gives you the most flexibility, it can also be the riskiest. If your independent method doesn't accurately verify accounts, it may negatively impact your transaction approval rates in the long run.\n\n\n### Business versus personal bank accounts\n\nIf you choose to verify bank accounts using the network check, micro-transfers, or independent check methods, one of the fields we require you to collect is the account ownership type, which can be either business or personal. While you can process transactions on both types of accounts, it's important to record the type for each account accurately. If you submit a transaction with the wrong account ownership type, the bank may decline the transaction request; this is commonly referred to as a return.\n\n\n### PayPal Company ID and Company Name\n\nAnother reason for a return may be that your customer has a debit block in place. If a transaction is returned for this reason, you'll be charged a return fee and will need to ask your customer to call their bank to get the debit block lifted from their bank account. Since ACH Direct Debit transactions are submitted to the ACH Network with the PayPal Company Name and Company ID in the NACHA file, they will need to provide their bank with the following Company IDs:\n\n\n- PAYPALMTCU\n- PAYPALMTBU\n\nIn addition, your customers may see \"PAYPAL,\" \"PAYPALMTCU,\" or \"PAYPALMTBU\" in the transaction descriptor in their statements.\n\n\n## Processing\n\nACH is a batch-based system, meaning that transactions do not take place in real-time, and authorizations or captures do not exist as they do with credit card transactions. This makes it more difficult to verify whether funds will be available in the account at the time the transaction takes place. As a result, debit entries can be returned by the customer's bank for insufficient funds or a variety of other reasons. Because of this, the settlement and funding timeline is different from standard credit cards.\n\n\n### Settlement and funding timeline\n\nBefore we disburse funds to your account, we wait for 3 business days for any returns that the customer's bank sends through the ACH network. If we are notified in that timeframe that the transaction has been returned and can't be processed, the transaction is declined and the funds will not be disbursed. It will be up to you to follow-up with your customer for payment. If the transaction is not returned to us in 3 business days, we assume the transaction is valid and will disburse funds to your bank account which takes 2 additional business days, totaling 5 business days. Returns can be received after the 3 business day timeframe; in those cases, we deduct the amount from the next business day's disbursement.\n\n\n**NOTE**\n We will disburse ACH funds together with your credit card transactions – you will only see one deposit that includes both credit card and ACH funds.\n\n \n\n\n### Transaction statuses\n\n\n- **Settlement Pending**: The transaction was successfully submitted to the banking network\n- **Failed**: An error occurred when submitting the transaction\n- **Settlement Declined**: The transaction was returned by the banking network in the 3 business days timeframe and the funds will not be disbursed to you – a $5 USD fee will apply\n- **Settled**: The transaction was not returned in the 3 business day timeframe and the funds will be disbursed to you. In the event that we receive a return after a transaction has \"settled,\" the status will remain \"settled\"\n- **Payment Method Blocked**: The payment method was blocked because there is a pending Micro-transfer or the verification process failed during Micro-transfer\n\nHere's a more detailed timeline for what the ACH transaction lifecycle looks like:\n\n\n##### Day of transaction\n\n\n- The transaction is processed and is immediately given theSettlement Pendingstatus\n\n\n##### 1-2 business days\n\n\n- If the transaction hasn't been returned by the banking network, the transaction will remain in theSettlement Pendingstatus\n- If the banking network returns the transaction, it will be moved to aSettlement Declinedstatus, and you will be notified\n\n\n##### 3rd business day\n\n\n- If the transaction has not been returned by the end of the day, the transaction will be considered good and given theSettledstatus\n- If the transaction is returned, it will be moved to aSettlement Declinedstatus\n- Regardless of the result, you will be notified of the status change on this day\n\n\n##### 4th business day\n\n\n- We disburse allSettledfunds to your account\n\n\n##### 5th business day\n\n\n- Disbursed funds should be reflected in your bank account alongside any disbursed credit card transactions from that day\n\n\n### Fees\n\nTransactions processed with ACH have an additional processing fee, which can be found in your pricing agreement. A $5 USD fee will be charged for any returns or disputes.\n\n\n### Voids and Refunds\n\nACH transactions can't be voided at any point in the transaction lifecycle. Refunds can be processed, but only on transactions with the Settled status. If you want to cancel a transaction, you'll need to wait for the transaction to settle and then issue a refund. If you issue a full refund, we will return the fees from the original transaction.\n\n\n\nIf you would like to enable the ability to void transactions, contact support. The void timeline will depend on the specific circumstances of the transaction.\n\nFor more details [refer](/braintree/articles/control-panel/transactions/refunds-voids-credits#voids) to Voids.\n\n\n### Returns\n\nACH transactions can be returned for a variety of reasons. Although a majority of returns are received in 3 business days, returns can be received after disbursement. In those cases, we deduct the amount from the next business day's disbursement and will display the transaction with an ACH Return Code followed by Failed After Settlement in the Control Panel. Below is a list of common ACH return codes and associated descriptions for your reference. Please refer to NACHA [http://www.nachaoperatingrulesonline.org/](http://www.nachaoperatingrulesonline.org/) for a more comprehensive list.\n\n\n- R01: Insufficient funds\n- R02: Account closed\n- R03: No account/unable to locate account\n- R07: Authorization revoked by customer\n- R08: Payment stopped\n- R10: Customer advises unauthorized, improper, ineligible, or part of an incomplete transaction\n- R29: Not authorized by corporate customer\n\n\n#### Rejects\n\nACH transactions can also be rejected by the bank processor if there are errors in the transaction details, such as an invalid routing number etc. When this occurs, the transaction is returned with the return code RJCT, along with additional information about the reason for the rejection.\n\nTo resolve a rejected transaction, refer to [ACH Reject Reason](https://developer.paypal.com/braintree/docs/reference/response/transaction/ruby/#ach_reject_reason) for guidance on how to fix and resubmit the transaction.\n\n\n### Blocked Bank Accounts\n\nTo comply with NACHA rules and minimize fraud, return fees, and other risks, we block bank accounts that receive certain ACH return codes. NACHA rules require that originators of ACH debits review and confirm the validity of a bank account or take corrective action before reinitiating a debit after a return (except for insufficient funds). Below is how we handle blocked accounts based on specific ACH return codes.\n\n**R02 and R03 Return Codes**   Bank accounts with return codes R02 (\"Bank Account Closed\") or R03 (\"No Account/Unable to Locate Account\") are blocked globally. This means no merchant, including yourself, will be able to transact with or vault these accounts. Any attempts to do so will trigger an error message.\n\n\n- Contact the customer to verify their bank account is still active and the account details and associated information (e.g., routing number) are correct.\n\n\n- If the bank account is no longer valid or active, ask the customer to provide an updated payment method for future transactions.\n\n\n- If you believe the account was blocked in error, please [contact us](https://developer.paypal.com/braintree/help/acceptPaymentTypes) to request an unblock.\n\n\n\n**R29 Return Code**   Bank accounts with return code R29 (\"Corporate Customer Advises Not Authorized\") are blocked **only for the merchant account who received the return code**. Other merchant accounts can still transact with this account. You will be unable to debit this account, and attempts will result in an error message.\n\n\n- Contact the customer to confirm that PayPal’s Company IDs (PAYPALMTCU, PAYPALMTBU) are added to their bank's allowlist and any authorization restrictions on the account have been lifted.\n\n\n- Once the customer confirms these updates, or if you suspect the block was a mistake, please [contact us](https://developer.paypal.com/braintree/help/acceptPaymentTypes) to request an unblock.\n\n\n\n\n\n\n### Disputes\n\nCustomers can report disputes to their bank for unauthorized transactions – commonly referred to as unauths. This window varies depending on the type of bank account they have.\n\n\n- **Business bank accounts** : 2 calendar days\n\n\n- **Personal bank accounts** : 60 calendar days\n\n\n\nDisputes reported within the given time window are automatically awarded to the customer (in compliance with the [Federal Reserve Regulation E](https://www.federalreserve.gov/bankinforeg/regecg.htm) ) and require no action from you. A non-refundable fee will be charged for each dispute for unauthorized transactions. We recommend working directly with the customer to resolve any disputes that you believe were made in error.\n\n\n#### Extended Dispute Periods\n\nCustomers may still dispute a transaction after the standard 60-day window. Under NACHA guidelines, unauthorized [warranty claims](https://www.nacha.org/rules/limitation-warranty-claims) can be raised:\n\n\n- **Business bank accounts:** Up to 1 year\n\n\n- **Personal bank accounts:** Up to 2 years\n\n\n\nIn such cases:\n\n\n- **Bank Request for Proof of Authorization:** The customer's bank (RDFI) will request proof of authorization from the originating bank (ODFI). The ODFI must respond to this request within 10 business days\n\n\n- **ODFI Outreach:** If the ODFI contacts us regarding the dispute, we will provide the mandate text and other relevant details submitted during [tokenization](https://developer.paypal.com/braintree/docs/guides/ach/client-side/javascript/v3/#tokenize-bank-account-information) or transaction as evidence or reach out to you to provide proof of authorization.\n\n\n- **Outcome:** If the Proof of Authorization does not validate that the transaction was authorized by the customer (e.g. name mismatch), the RDFI has the right issue an unauthorized return and a non-refundable fee will be charged. In such cases, a non-refundable fee will be applied, and the RDFI's decision is final, meaning you cannot challenge or dispute the outcome further once their determination has been made.\n\n\n\nTo minimize the risk of extended disputes, ensure compliance with [our guidance](https://developer.paypal.com/braintree/docs/guides/ach/client-side/javascript/v3/#show-required-authorization-language) for authorization language, properly display and submit the proof of authorization during transactions and collect [device data](https://developer.paypal.com/braintree/docs/guides/ach/client-side/javascript/v3/#device-data-collection).\n\n\n\nRecurring billing and vaultingBank accounts can be vaulted. However, Braintree's recurring billing currently does not support repeat payments with bank accounts.\n\n\n## Setup\n\nTo add ACH Direct Debit to your Braintree integration, you'll need to update your application code and make sure ACH Direct Debit is enabled in your Control Panel.
