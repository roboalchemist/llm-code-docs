  the beginning of the next cycle.

  </span></p></span></div></div><p class="markdown-p"><span>

  or applying a discount.
</span></p><h3 id="proration-with-add-ons-and-discounts" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Proration with add-ons and discounts</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#proration-with-add-ons-and-discounts" aria-label="Proration with add-ons and discounts"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  In order to adjust a subscription’s price, you can either use
  </span><a href="/braintree/articles/guides/recurring-billing/add-ons-discounts"><span>add-ons/discounts</span></a><span>, or
  directly
  </span><a href="/braintree/articles/guides/recurring-billing/subscriptions#updating-a-subscription"><span>update the subscription</span></a><span>
  and change the price. When you make an update to the subscription, the price change will be
  reflected for the remainder of its billing cycles or until another change is made. Alternatively,
  when you use add-ons/discounts, you must define how many billing cycles should be adjusted.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>

  amount left on the current billing cycle.

  </span></p></span></div></div><h3 id="enable-proration-on-upgrades" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Enable proration on upgrades</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#enable-proration-on-upgrades" aria-label="Enable proration on upgrades"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>

</span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>Log into the </span><a href="https://www.braintreegateway.com/login"><span>Control Panel</span></a></li><li><span>

  If the price of a subscription increases in the middle of the billing cycle and you have enabled

</span></p><h3 id="enable-proration-on-downgrades" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Enable proration on downgrades</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#enable-proration-on-downgrades" aria-label="Enable proration on downgrades"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  If you edit the subscription price to a lower dollar amount or apply a discount, it is defined as
  a </span><strong><span>downgrade</span></strong><span>. To enable proration on downgrades:
</span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>Log into the </span><a href="https://www.braintreegateway.com/login"><span>Control Panel</span></a></li><li><span>
    Navigate to </span><strong><span>Settings</span></strong><span> &gt; </span><strong><span>Account Settings</span></strong><span> &gt;
    </span><strong><span>Recurring Billing</span></strong></li><li><span>Click </span><strong><span>Options</span></strong></li><li><span>Check the box next to </span><em><span>Enable proration on downgrades</span></em></li></ol><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  If the price of a subscription decreases in the middle of the billing cycle and you have enabled
  proration on downgrades, then we will subtract the prorated amount from the customer’s
  subscription balance. A customer’s balance should be $0 if they pay their bill on time, so you
  should see a negative balance for them in the gateway.
</span></p><p class="markdown-p"><span>

  subscription. If the downgrade gave them a negative balance, their payment method won’t be charged
  until their balance exceeds $0.

You may have to wait up to two days before all three retries have finished executing and the subscription shows as Past Due. If the transaction has been PROCESSOR*DECLINED or GATEWAY_REJECTED, the subscription status will change to Past Due immediately. After a subscription is marked Past Due, we will try billing the subscription at least two more times. These two retries would occur whenever recurring billing runs but you can choose to specify the interval after which to retry in the control panel. The default plan after these two retries fail is retrying the subscription once per billing cycle. This default backup plan can be changed in the control panel to one of [these options](/braintree/articles/guides/recurring-billing/recurring-advanced-settings#if-above-retry-fails--try-again-after).\n\n\n### Setting up retry logic\n\nTo find the options for automatic retries:\n\n\n- Log into the[Control Panel](https://www.braintreegateway.com/login)\n- Click on the gear icon in the top right corner\n- Click**Account Settings**from the drop-down menu\n- Scroll to the**Recurring Billing**section\n- Next to**Recurring Billing**, click the**Options**link\n- Check the box next toAutomatically retry failed transactions\n- Make your selections;[learn more](#retry-logic-options)\n- Click the**Save Recurring Billing Options**button\n\n\n#### Retry logic options\n\n\n##### After a subscription is past due, retry the subscription after…\n\nHere’s where you can specify how long you want to wait to retry the charge after it initially fails or is declined.
