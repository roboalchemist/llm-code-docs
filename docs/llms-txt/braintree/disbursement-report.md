markdown-h2"><span>Document Version 1.1</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#document-version-1-1" aria-label="Document Version 1.1"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><a href="/braintree/articles/br/reporting-reconciliation/disbursement-report#changelog"><span>Changelog</span></a></p><h2 id="report-definition" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Report Definition</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#report-definition" aria-label="Report Definition"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  To reconcile your daily deposits from PayPal and Braintree with your system of records to clear
  your receivables, you will need to use the Disbursement Report. This report will contain both
  summarized and event-level detail of every payment event that impacted a deposit from PayPal and
  Braintree to your deposit bank account.
</span></p><h2 id="report-format" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Report format</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#report-format" aria-label="Report format"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><p class="markdown-p"><span>The Disbursement Report contains 4 sections:</span></p><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>Header</span></li><li><span>Summary</span></li><li><span>Detail</span></li><li><span>Footer</span></li></ul><h3 id="header" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Header</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#header" aria-label="Header"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  The </span><strong><span>Header</span></strong><span> section will contain report configuration and status information. This
  is where you will find information like the timezone of the data in the report, or report name.
</span></p><h3 id="summary" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Summary</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#summary" aria-label="Summary"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  The </span><strong><span>Summary</span></strong><span> section will contain summarized data about the disbursement batches
  from the reporting period. Each disbursement batch will appear as a line-item in this section and
  will have information like the total amount disbursed per merchant account, the transfer ID of the
  disbursement, and the bank account to which the funds were disbursed.
</span></p><h3 id="detail" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Detail</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#detail" aria-label="Detail"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  The </span><strong><span>Detail</span></strong><span> section will contain line-item detail for all the disbursement
  impacting events (e.g. transactions, disputes, etc.).
</span></p><h3 id="footer" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Footer</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#footer" aria-label="Footer"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  This section will provide data that can be used to verify that the number of records ingested into
  your system matches the number of records in the file. It will also include the total number of
  files generated for the day and the sequence number of the files within that set.
</span></p><h2 id="how-to-use-the-report" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>How to Use the Report</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#how-to-use-the-report" aria-label="How to Use the Report"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h2><h3 id="reconciling-deposit-amount-to-funding-account-in-paypal" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Reconciling deposit amount to funding account in PayPal</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#reconciling-deposit-amount-to-funding-account-in-paypal" aria-label="Reconciling deposit amount to funding account in PayPal"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  Below are the steps to reconcile the line-item deposit records from PayPal on your bank statement
  with the Disbursement Report:
</span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>
    Match each deposit amount from PayPal on your bank statement with the amounts found in the
    </span><em><span>Net Disbursed Amount</span></em><span> columns of the </span><strong><span>Summary</span></strong><span> section of the
    Disbursement Report
  </span></li><li><span>
    Locate the </span><em><span>Funding Account</span></em><span> for each deposit line-item in the
    </span><strong><span>Summary</span></strong><span> section. For Brazil, this value will always be a PayPal Account Number
    because both PayPal Wallet and Credit/Debit Card transactions are disbursed through a PayPal
    account.
  </span></li><li><span>
    Use the values found in the </span><em><span>Funding Account</span></em><span> column of the
    </span><strong><span>Summary</span></strong><span> section for each line-item deposit to identify the rows in the
    </span><strong><span>Detail</span></strong><span> section that belong to each deposit.
  </span></li><li><span>
    Sum the values found in </span><em><span>Net Disbursed</span></em><span> column for each group of rows filtered by
    </span><em><span>Funding Account.</span></em><span> The sum for each group will match the amounts found in the
    </span><strong><span>Summary</span></strong><span> sections
  </span></li></ol><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  Once you have confirmed that all the disbursement impacting items (e.g. transactions, disputes,
  etc) are present in the report, you can begin reconciling the items by </span><em><span>Record Type</span></em><span> as
  outlined in the following sections.
</span></p><h3 id="reconciling-standard-sales-and-refunds" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Reconciling standard sales and refunds</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#reconciling-standard-sales-and-refunds" aria-label="Reconciling standard sales and refunds"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  To reconcile standard sales and refunds records that have been disbursed to your bank account,
  follow the below methodology:
</span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>Locate records where </span><em><span>Record Type</span></em><span> is either </span><em><span>Sale</span></em><span> or </span><em><span>Refund</span></em><span>.</span></li><li><span>
    With the </span><em><span>Sale</span></em><span> and </span><em><span>Refund</span></em><span> records selected, iterate through the set using the
    </span><em><span>Record ID</span></em><span> field to locate the </span><em><span>Transaction ID</span></em><span>, and ingest the following data at
    a minimum:
    </span><ul class="list list--disc"><li><em><span>Disbursement Date</span></em><span> – The date the installment was disbursed</span></li><li><em><span>Transfer ID</span></em><span> – The unique ID include in the descriptor on the bank transfer. Summing
        all the </span><em><span>Net Disbursed</span></em><span> items in the disbursement report by </span><em><span>Transfer ID</span></em><span> will
        equal the deposit amount in your bank account.
      </span></li><li><em><span>Net Disbursed</span></em><span> – The amount of the installment net fees, in disbursement currency,
        that was included in the deposit.
      </span></li></ul></li></ol><h3 id="reconciling-installments" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Reconciling installments</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#reconciling-installments" aria-label="Reconciling installments"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>
  To reconcile the installments that have been disbursed to your bank account, follow the below
  methodology:
</span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>
    Locate records where </span><em><span>Record Type</span></em><span> = </span><strong><span>installments</span></strong><span> and
    </span><em><span>Record Subtype</span></em><span> = </span><strong><span>disbursements</span></strong><span>.
  </span></li><li><span>
    With the installment disbursement records selected, iterate through the installments using the
    </span><em><span>Record ID</span></em><span> field to locate the </span><em><span>Installment ID</span></em><span>, and ingest the following data at
    a minimum:
    </span><ul class="list list--disc"><li><em><span>Disbursement Date</span></em><span> – The date the installment was disbursed</span></li><li><em><span>Transfer ID</span></em><span> – The unique ID include in the descriptor on the bank transfer. Summing
        all the </span><em><span>Net Disbursed</span></em><span> items in the disbursement report by </span><em><span>Transfer ID</span></em><span> will
        equal the deposit amount in your bank account.
      </span></li><li><em><span>Net Disbursed</span></em><span> – The amount of installment net fees, in disbursement currency, that
        was included in the deposit.
      </span></li></ul></li><li><span>
    Locate records where </span><em><span>Record Type</span></em><span> = </span><strong><span>Installments</span></strong><span> and
    </span><em><span>Record Subtype</span></em><span> = </span><strong><span>Adjustments</span></strong><span>, and repeat step 2, but for the
    adjustment records in your system of record.
  </span></li></ol><h3 id="reconciling-disputes" class="test_h3 markdown-h3"><span>Reconciling disputes</span><a class="icon icon--link hash-link" href="#reconciling-disputes" aria-label="Reconciling disputes"><img src="https://www.paypalobjects.com/btdevdoc/bundles/4.3.24/_assets/anchor-PKYVW2VT.svg" alt="AnchorIcon"/></a></h3><p class="markdown-p"><span>To reconcile disputes that have impacted your disbursement, follow the below methodology:</span></p><ol class="list list--num"><li><span>Locate records where </span><em><span>Record Type</span></em><span> = </span><strong><span>Dispute</span></strong><span>.</span></li><li><span>
    With the dispute records selected, iterate through disputes using the </span><em><span>Dispute ID</span></em><span> column
    to identify the dispute.
  </span></li><li><span>
    Match the value in the </span><em><span>Net Disbursed</span></em><span> column with the disputed amount.
    </span><ul class="list list--disc"><li><span>
        If </span><em><span>Record Subtype</span></em><span> = </span><strong><span>Chargeback</span></strong><span> then the dispute should have a
        status of </span><strong><span>Lost</span></strong><span> and should be a negative amount
      </span></li><li><span>
        If </span><em><span>Record Subtype</span></em><span> = </span><strong><span>Chargeback reversal</span></strong><span> then the dispute should
        have a status of </span><strong><span>Won</span></strong><span> and be a positive amount
      </span></li></ul></li></ol><h2 id="data-dictionaries-for-disbursement-report-sections" class="test_h2 markdown-h2"><span>Data dictionaries for Disbursement Report sections</span><a class="icon Report\n\n\n## Document Version 1.1\n\n[Changelog](#changelog)\n\n\n## Report Definition\n\nTo reconcile your daily deposits from PayPal and Braintree with your system of records to clear your receivables, you will need to use the Disbursement Report. This report will contain both summarized and event-level detail of every payment event that impacted a deposit from PayPal and Braintree to your deposit bank account.\n\n\n## Report format\n\nThe Disbursement Report contains 4 sections:\n\n\n- Header\n- Summary\n- Detail\n- Footer\n\n\n### Header\n\nThe **Header** section will contain report configuration and status information. This is where you will find information like the timezone of the data in the report, or report name.\n\n\n### Summary\n\nThe **Summary** section will contain summarized data about the disbursement batches from the reporting period. Each disbursement batch will appear as a line-item in this section and will have information like the total amount disbursed per merchant account, the transfer ID of the disbursement, and the bank account to which the funds were disbursed.\n\n\n### Detail\n\nThe **Detail** section will contain line-item detail for all the disbursement impacting events (e.g. transactions, disputes, etc.).\n\n\n### Footer\n\nThis section will provide data that can be used to verify that the number of records ingested into your system matches the number of records in the file. It will also include the total number of files generated for the day and the sequence number of the files within that set.\n\n\n## How to Use the Report\n\n\n### Reconciling deposit amount to funding account in PayPal\n\nBelow are the steps to reconcile the line-item deposit records from PayPal on your bank statement with the Disbursement Report:\n\n\n- Match each deposit amount from PayPal on your bank statement with the amounts found in theNet Disbursed Amountcolumns of the**Summary**section of the Disbursement Report\n- Locate theFunding Accountfor each deposit line-item in the**Summary**section. For Brazil, this value will always be a PayPal Account Number because both PayPal Wallet and Credit/Debit Card transactions are disbursed through a PayPal account.\n- Use the values found in theFunding Accountcolumn of the**Summary**section for each line-item deposit to identify the rows in the**Detail**section that belong to each deposit.\n- Sum the values found inNet Disbursedcolumn for each group of rows filtered byFunding Account.The sum for each group will match the amounts found in the**Summary**sections\n\nOnce you have confirmed that all the disbursement impacting items (e.g. transactions, disputes, etc) are present in the report, you can begin reconciling the items by Record Type as outlined in the following sections.\n\n\n### Reconciling standard sales and refunds\n\nTo reconcile standard sales and refunds records that have been disbursed to your bank account, follow the below methodology:\n\n\n- Locate records whereRecord Typeis eitherSaleorRefund.\n- With theSaleandRefundrecords selected, iterate through the set using theRecord IDfield to locate theTransaction ID, and ingest the following data at a minimum:\n- Disbursement Date– The date the installment was disbursed\n- Transfer ID– The unique ID include in the descriptor on the bank transfer. Summing all theNet Disburseditems in the disbursement report byTransfer IDwill equal the deposit amount in your bank account.\n- Net Disbursed– The amount of the installment net fees, in disbursement currency, that was included in the deposit.\n\n\n\n\n### Reconciling installments\n\nTo reconcile the installments that have been disbursed to your bank account, follow the below methodology:\n\n\n- Locate records whereRecord Type=**installments**andRecord Subtype=**disbursements**.\n- With the installment disbursement records selected, iterate through the installments using theRecord IDfield to locate theInstallment ID, and ingest the following data at a minimum:\n- Disbursement Date– The date the installment was disbursed\n- Transfer ID– The unique ID include in the descriptor on the bank transfer. Summing all theNet Disburseditems in the disbursement report byTransfer IDwill equal the deposit amount in your bank account.\n- Net Disbursed– The amount of installment net fees, in disbursement currency, that was included in the deposit.\n\n\n- Locate records whereRecord Type=**Installments**andRecord Subtype=**Adjustments**, and repeat step 2, but for the adjustment records in your system of record.\n\n\n### Reconciling disputes\n\nTo reconcile disputes that have impacted your disbursement, follow the below methodology:\n\n\n- Locate records whereRecord Type=**Dispute**.\n- With the dispute records selected, iterate through disputes using theDispute IDcolumn to identify the dispute.\n- Match the value in theNet Disbursedcolumn with the disputed amount.\n- IfRecord Subtype=**Chargeback**then the dispute should have a status of**Lost**and should be a negative amount\n- IfRecord Subtype=**Chargeback reversal**then the dispute should have a status of**Won**and be a positive amount\n\n\n\n\n## Data dictionaries for Disbursement Report sections\n\n\n### Report Header Data Dictionary\n\n| Field Name | Description |\n| --- | --- |\n| RH | RH is short for \"Report Header.\" All rows in the Header section will have the value**RH**in theRHcolumn to indicate the rows belong to the Header section. |\n| Report name | The name of the Report. |\n| Report status | Status of the report.
