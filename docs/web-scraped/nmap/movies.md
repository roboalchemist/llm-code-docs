For reasons unknown, Hollywood has decided that Nmap is _the_ tool to show whenever hacking scenes are needed. At least it is a lot more realistic than silly 3D animation approach used in many previous movies (e.g. "hacking the Gibson" on Hackers, or the much worse portrayals on Swordfish). We always like to see Nmap in the movies, so we have catalogued known instances here.

If you catch Nmap in another flick, please [mail Fyodor](mailto:fyodor@nmap.org).

Update (April 21, 2020): I (Fyodor) am way behind on adding new movies. Sorry about that!

Movie script writers, artists, and digital asset managers are also welcome to [email Fyodor](mailto:fyodor@nmap.org) for advice. We've been pleased to help out to make a number of movies slightly more realistic and entertaining by improving the hacking scenes.

This page only covers movies, we have a separate page coving [news articles, reviews, books, and popular culture references](https://nmap.org/nmap_inthenews.html).

While Nmap had been used in some previous obscure movies, it was [The Matrix Reloaded](http://www.amazon.com/dp/B0000AXE8I?tag=secbks-20) ([Wikipedia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Matrix_Reloaded), [IMDB](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0234215/), [Amazon](http://www.amazon.com/dp/B0000AXE8I?tag=secbks-20)) which really turned Nmap into a movie star!

We have all seen many movies like [Hackers](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0113243/) which pass off ridiculous 3D animated eye-candy scenes as hacking. So Fyodor [was shocked](http://seclists.org/nmap-hackers/2003/Apr-Jun/0010.html) to find that Trinity does it properly in [The Matrix Reloaded](http://www.amazon.com/dp/B0000AXE8I?tag=secbks-20). Needing to hack the city power grid, she whips out [Nmap](http://nmap.org/) version [2.54BETA25](http://seclists.org/nmap-hackers/2001/Apr-Jun/0005.html), uses it to find a vulnerable SSH server, and then proceeds to exploit it using the [SSH1 CRC32](http://packetstormsecurity.org/advisories/bindview/adv_ssh1crc.txt) exploit from 2001. Shame on the city for being vulnerable ([timing notes](http://insecure.org/stf/neiljk.html)).

A video of the exploit is [available on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0PxTAn4g20U) or as [matrix-nmap.mp4](https://nmap.org/movies/matrix/matrix-nmap.mp4). Click on the following thumbnails for higher resolution or view [more pictures here](https://nmap.org/movies/matrix/).

Oliver Stone's 2016 [Snowden](http://www.amazon.com/dp/B01LTHO65M?tag=secbks-20) film ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snowden_(film)), [IMDB](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt3774114/), [Amazon](http://www.amazon.com/dp/B01LTHO65M?tag=secbks-20)) dramatizes Edward Snowden's massive leak of classified documents revealing NSA widespread surveillance of U.S. citizens. In one early scene, Snowden is given a network security challenge at a CIA training class which is expected to take 5 to 8 hours. [Who Am I—No System is Safe](http://www.amazon.com/dp/B00NW8C1GS?tag=secbks-20) ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Who_Am_I_%E2%80%93_No_System_Is_Safe), [IMDB](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt3042408/), [Amazon](http://www.amazon.com/dp/B00NW8C1GS?tag=secbks-20)) is a 2014 German cyber-thriller about dueling hacker groups (patterned after Anonymous) vying to commit the most audacious system compromises and pranks. During an initiation scene 13:40 into the movie, the protagonist Benjamin proves his skills by compromising the local power company and causing a brief blackout. He does so by running an [Nmap Scripting Engine (NSE)](http://nmap.org/book/nse.html) script that he has presumably written. The script is named iec-backdoor.nse, referencing the [International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Electrotechnical_Commission) standards which control utility power grid equipment. [Broken Saints](http://www.amazon.com/dp/B000FKO3RQ?tag=secbks-20) ([Wikipedia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broken_Saints), [IMDB](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0451002/), [Amazon](http://www.amazon.com/dp/B000FKO3RQ?tag=secbks-20)) is an award-winning animated film series ([motion comic](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motion_comic)) produced in 24 chapters first published online between 2001 and 2003. It is a complex, 12-hour tale of four strangers from "the quiet corners of the globe" connected by a vision they all receive of coming evil.

You don't really need to watch Chapter 1 first, but it is [available here](https://www.youtube.com/user/brokensaint#p/u/28/JxE9EODS588). The Nmap cameo was first discovered by David Alexander. In the Japanese drama series [Bloody Monday](http://wiki.d-addicts.com/Bloody_Monday), a hacker named Falcon must use his computer skills to unravel a mysterious project “Bloody Monday” and prevent a biological terrorist attack from devastating Tokyo. Nmap is used in multiple episodes, starting with Season One, Episode One. You can also find Nmap used about 20 seconds into [this clip](http://www.wat.tv/video/b4b-28co1_2jvup_.html) (Season 2, Episode 4). Other security tools [portrayed realistically](https://nmap.org/movies/bloodymonday/bm-sadmind.jpg) include Netcat, Rainbow Crack, and an Sadmind exploit.
