What is a Heatmap?
------------------

A Heatmap or Heat map is a visualization tool that makes it easy to analyze aggregated information about how users interact with the website.

Clarity tracks all visitor clicks and scrolls on mobile, desktop, and tablet and automatically generates a heat map. This data helps you identify the most engaged areas and figure out gaps.

There's no creation delay. Heat maps return within a matter of seconds. Clarity supports [Click maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/click-maps), [Scroll maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/scroll-maps), [Area maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/area-maps), [Conversion maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/conversion-maps), and [Attention maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/attention-maps).

Note

There's no minimum traffic limitation to generate a heat map.

Heat maps can be generated for a single [page](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/setup-and-installation/glossary-of-terms) or group of [pages](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/setup-and-installation/glossary-of-terms).

[](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-overview#why-use-clarity-heatmaps)
Why use Clarity heatmaps?
-------------------------

Heatmaps help you look at user behavior across sessions so you can easily identify what works and what doesn't on your page, such as:

*   Find out if you have the right hierarchy of elements on your page by looking at [scroll maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/scroll-maps).
*   See if your CTAs are getting any attention by looking at [click maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/click-maps).
*   Discover the clicks that generate most revenue by looking at [conversion maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/conversion-maps).

[](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-overview#how-do-heatmaps-work-for-my-website)
How do Heatmaps work for my website?
------------------------------------

Heat maps are created from the data already collected by Clarity. Clarity aggregates information on clicks/scroll reaches across different page views based on the filters you choose to generate the heatmap.

[](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-overview#heatmaps-zero-state)
### Heatmaps zero state

Note

You can view zero state Heatmaps only if there's no applied [Visited URL filter](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/filters/clarity-filters#filtering-by-path).

After onboarding, navigate to Heatmaps to view a list of popular pages. Adjust your [Filters](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/filters/clarity-filters) and [Segments](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/filters/clarity-segments) to view according to your preferences. The pages are sorted based on the highest number of sessions, with their page titles, URLs, and visit counts displayed.

A page title is the text shown on a browser tab and in search results, offering a brief description of a webpage's content. To improve SEO, ensure page titles are clear, relevant, and aligned with the URL. If a page lacks a title, adding one can enhance search visibility and user experience. The **& more** label signifies that multiple page titles are linked to a single URL.

![Image 14: Heatmaps zero state.](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/media/heatmaps-zero-state.png)

You can also enter a URL and or part of URL to start viewing the Heatmaps. Learn more on how to achieve this with [path filters and its supporting operators](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/filters/path-filters-operators).

![Image 15: View Heatmaps by entering URL.](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/media/heatmaps-add-url-group-of-pages.png)

[](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-overview#url-parameters)
#### URL parameters

When you toggle the URL parameters and select a URL, you can view the heatmap that matches the URL and parameters exactly. When toggled off, you see a heatmap that start with the specified value, with or without parameters.

![Image 16: View Heatmaps with and without parameters.](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/media/url-parameters.gif)

If you've already applied a Visited URL filter in the Recordings or Dashboard sections, the heatmap for that URL appears in the Heatmaps section, instead of the zero state.

[](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-overview#types-of-heatmaps)
Types of Heatmaps
-----------------

There are different types of heat maps based on the kind of interaction.

![Image 17: Heatmaps types.](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/media/heatmap-types.png)

[](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-overview#click-maps)
### Click maps

Clicking on a heatmap shows where users are clicking on the page. It gives information about the most clicked elements and how many clicks on these elements.

Clarity click maps are built using an advanced methodology of elements-based user clicks instead of the absolute mouse position. Click maps are precise in calculating user heat in scenarios like different viewport sizes, responsive site design, and page states.

There are [different types of Click maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/click-maps#different-types-of-click-maps):

*   All clicks
*   Dead clicks
*   Rage clicks
*   Error clicks
*   First clicks
*   Last clicks

![Image 18: Click maps.](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/media/clickmaps.png)

Tip

Learn more about [click maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/click-maps).

[](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-overview#scroll-maps)
### Scroll maps

Clarity Scroll maps track how far users scrolled down a page (hot being popular and cold being unpopular). It gives information about:

*   Percentages of users who scroll up to a particular section on your webpage.

*   **Average fold**: Content seen above the fold is based on the users accessing your site. This fold indicates the average location visible on a webpage before the user starts scrolling.

This type of heat map is most useful when representing the most and least viewed parts of a webpage. An average fold is calculated to visualize how much your users can see on average before they scroll down.

![Image 19: Scroll maps.](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/media/scrollmaps.png)

Tip

Learn more about [Scroll maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/scroll-maps).

[](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-overview#area-maps)
### Area maps

Clarity Area maps display the total clicks for all the elements within a selected area.

Unlike a click map, you can choose the area and view the clicks for all the elements within it.

![Image 20: Area maps.](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/media/areamaps.png)

Tip

Learn more about [Area maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/area-maps).

[](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-overview#conversion-maps)
### Conversion maps

Conversion maps provide specific data on purchase rate of your E-Commerce sites. Using conversion maps you can see what clicks lead to best conversions, which elements caused highest purchase prices, and what parts of your page are less successful.

![Image 21: conversion maps.](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/media/conversion-maps.png)

Tip

Learn more about [Conversion maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/conversion-maps).

[](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-overview#attention-maps)
### Attention maps

Attention maps display the amount of time users spent on each part of the page.

![Image 22: attention maps.](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/media/attention-maps.png)

Tip

Learn more about [Attention maps](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/attention-maps).

[](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-overview#how-can-i-use-heatmaps-for-higher-conversion-rates)
How can I use Heatmaps for higher conversion rates?
---------------------------------------------------

*   You can use the [heatmaps compare](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/clarity/heatmaps-compare) feature to observe how site changes impacted your conversions.
*   You can identify the most engaging areas of your page, allowing you to focus your efforts more effectively.
*   You can use heatmaps to easily determine if your site's hierarchy and order of elements make sense to users.
*   You can gain insights from click data to understand which elements resonate with your users, helping you make informed design decisions.
*   You can use scroll maps to easily identify what content your users miss on your page, ensuring that important elements are always within reach.
