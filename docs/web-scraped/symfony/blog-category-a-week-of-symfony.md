A weekly summary of everything that happened around Symfony development.

[March 2–8, 2026 A Week of Symfony #1001](https://symfony.com/blog/a-week-of-symfony-1001-march-2-8-2026)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 This week, Symfony [6.4.35](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-6-4-35-released), [7.4.7](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-7-4-7-released), and [8.0.7](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-8-0-7-released) maintenance versions were released. In addition, we introduced [featured listings](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-jobs-elevate-your-talent-search) for [Symfony job postings](https://symfony.com/jobs). Lastly, we published [AI skills for Symfony UX](https://symfony.com/blog/introducing-ai-skills-for-symfony-ux).

 March 8, 2026 [#A week of symfony](https://symfony.com/blog/category/a-week-of-symfony)

[February 23 – March 1, 2026 A Week of Symfony #1000](https://symfony.com/blog/a-week-of-symfony-1000-february-23-march-1-2026)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 This week, we celebrated the 1,000th issue of the "A Week of Symfony" blog series.

 March 1, 2026 [#A week of symfony](https://symfony.com/blog/category/a-week-of-symfony)❤️27👍6🚀14😄5🎉21

[February 16–22, 2026 A Week of Symfony #999](https://symfony.com/blog/a-week-of-symfony-999-february-16-22-2026)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 This week, development activity focused on misc fixes and improvements across all maintained Symfony branches. In addition, we published [more information](https://symfony.com/blog/symfonylive-berlin-2026-schedule-is-out-and-more-talks-are-coming) about the [SymfonyLive Berlin 2026](https://live.symfony.com/2026-berlin/) and [SymfonyLive Paris 2026](https://live.symfony.com/2026-paris/) conferences.

 February 22, 2026 [#A week of symfony](https://symfony.com/blog/category/a-week-of-symfony)👍2

[February 9–15, 2026 A Week of Symfony #998](https://symfony.com/blog/a-week-of-symfony-998-february-9-15-2026)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 This week, development activity was very intense for the upcoming Symfony 8.1 release, which introduced new features across the framework. Meanwhile, we shared more details about the upcoming [SymfonyLive Paris 2026](https://live.symfony.com/2025-paris/) conference. Finally, SymfonyCasts [announced](https://symfony.com/blog/new-symfonycasts-course-symfony-forms-the-basics) a new course about Symfony Forms.

 February 15, 2026 [#A week of symfony](https://symfony.com/blog/category/a-week-of-symfony)👍1

[February 2–8, 2026 A Week of Symfony #997](https://symfony.com/blog/a-week-of-symfony-997-february-2-8-2026)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 This week, the upcoming Symfony 8.1 development activity focused on controllers and attributes. In attributes, we added features like support for ["this" variable in IsGranted attribute](https://github.com/symfony/symfony/commit/cdc22f9b2c5f3276f9b5d89f4a6cd2d55e60fcde) and [support of expressions in MapRequestPayload validation groups](https://github.com/symfony/symfony/commit/386a8cc1c0b2809586a962b6f940063a58833792). Controllers added features to expose [controller metadata throughout the request lifecycle](https://github.com/symfony/symfony/commit/c778b8c36ff51d512341bd6c46ada5eed022752a), dispatch [events named after controller attributes](https://github.com/symfony/symfony/commit/2363764d72b958740fca0b00e529b036088af639), and new methods to [evaluate expressions or closures in controller attributes](https://github.com/symfony/symfony/commit/f5a236d9f1d106166adafc4d75caefe11c7dad16).

 February 8, 2026 [#A week of symfony](https://symfony.com/blog/category/a-week-of-symfony)👍1

[January 26 – February 1, 2026 A Week of Symfony #996](https://symfony.com/blog/a-week-of-symfony-996-january-26-february-1-2026)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 This week, development activity focused on adding new features to the upcoming Symfony 8.1 version. Meanwhile, we published [5.4.51](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-5-4-51-released), [6.4.33](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-6-4-33-released), [7.3.11](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-7-3-11-released), [7.4.5](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-7-4-5-released), and [8.0.5](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-8-0-5-released) versions to address a potential security vulnerability. Finally, we published an article about [hardening Symfony with recent security improvements](https://symfony.com/blog/hardening-symfony-recent-security-improvements).

 February 1, 2026 [#A week of symfony](https://symfony.com/blog/category/a-week-of-symfony)👍3

[January 19–25, 2026 A Week of Symfony #995](https://symfony.com/blog/a-week-of-symfony-995-january-19-25-2026)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 This week, Symfony celebrated the [Online January 2026](https://live.symfony.com/2026-online-january/) conference. Meanwhile, the upcoming Symfony 8.1 version added support for [persistent cURL handles](https://github.com/symfony/symfony/commit/a4d18c8dcebe19c2bb6f6ae04eb34e0f4a0a6d94) in HttpClient, enabled [mocking non-shared services in tests](https://github.com/symfony/symfony/commit/2ed74bda1da9b452a6555c51502bf9660f04f5a5), and updated ContainerConfigurator to allow [excluding items when importing services](https://github.com/symfony/symfony/commit/4b6d12dcef312a54b9876e1ca243bb4e37aa1e51). Lastly, Twig 3.23 was released with [new operators and destructuring support](https://symfony.com/blog/twig-3-23-introducing-new-operators-and-destructuring-support).

 January 25, 2026 [#A week of symfony](https://symfony.com/blog/category/a-week-of-symfony)❤️1

[January 12–18, 2026 A Week of Symfony #994](https://symfony.com/blog/a-week-of-symfony-994-january-12-18-2026)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 This week, Symfony development activity focused on [improving the HTTP Cache attribute](https://github.com/symfony/symfony/commit/12c438b4d8c9e64fe8128c9165281377402a2ee7) and making [some changes](https://github.com/symfony/symfony/commit/56c40dd31681029b41248319108e395ce8aebaea) to controller event attributes. Meanwhile, we published more information about the upcoming [SymfonyLive Paris 2026](https://live.symfony.com/2026-paris/) conference. Lastly, we [introduced the Symfony 8 certification](https://symfony.com/blog/introducing-the-symfony-8-certification).

 January 18, 2026 [#A week of symfony](https://symfony.com/blog/category/a-week-of-symfony)

[January 5–11, 2026 A Week of Symfony #993](https://symfony.com/blog/a-week-of-symfony-993-january-5-11-2026)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 This week, Symfony development activity focused on fixing bugs and updating tests to make them compatible with the newest PHPUnit versions. Meanwhile, the upcoming Symfony 8.1 release started adding new features, such as a [new attribute for interactive choice questions](https://github.com/symfony/symfony/commit/255ec541bef3a0bc30107d62d828b63333da7b93) in console commands. Finally, Symfony UX [introduced a new Toolkit package](https://symfony.com/blog/symfonyux-2-32-0-released).

 January 11, 2026 [#A week of symfony](https://symfony.com/blog/category/a-week-of-symfony)❤️1

[December 29, 2025 – January 4, 2026 A Week of Symfony #992](https://symfony.com/blog/a-week-of-symfony-992-december-29-2025-january-4-2026)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 This week, Symfony [6.4.31](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-6-4-31-released), [7.3.9](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-7-3-9-released), [7.4.3](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-7-4-3-released), and [8.0.3](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-8-0-3-released) maintenance versions were released. In addition, we published the [2025 year recap](https://symfony.com/blog/symfony-2025-year-in-review) blog post to review the main Symfony activity during the last year.

 January 4, 2026 [#A week of symfony](https://symfony.com/blog/category/a-week-of-symfony)❤️2
