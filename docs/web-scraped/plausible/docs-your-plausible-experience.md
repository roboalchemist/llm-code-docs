Markdown Content:
To use Plausible Analytics, you need to [register an account](https://plausible.io/register) and add [our tracking snippet](https://plausible.io/docs/plausible-script) to your site.

To explore the product, we offer you a free 30-day trial. You can test all the different features and use Plausible on multiple sites during the trial. No credit card is required to sign up.

Here are the steps you can take to get the most out of your Plausible experience:

Tracking and events[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#tracking-and-events "Direct link to Tracking and events")
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Set up goals to track conversions[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#set-up-goals-to-track-conversions "Direct link to Set up goals to track conversions")

Plausible allows you to track custom event goals like signups, purchases and anything else you can think of by using CSS class names or JavaScript. You can send custom properties alongside events too. And if you're an ecommerce store, you can send your revenue too. [See the instructions here](https://plausible.io/docs/custom-event-goals).

You can also enable some automated goals. Here are instructions on how to track [404 error pages](https://plausible.io/docs/error-pages-tracking-404), [file downloads](https://plausible.io/docs/file-downloads-tracking), [outbound link clicks](https://plausible.io/docs/outbound-link-click-tracking) and [form submissions](https://plausible.io/docs/form-submissions-tracking) in your Plausible dashboard.

After you've set up some events, you can create your first [marketing funnel](https://plausible.io/docs/funnel-analysis).

See full details on our [goal conversions and behavior analytics](https://plausible.io/docs/goal-conversions).

### Start using UTM tags in your marketing campaigns[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#start-using-utm-tags-in-your-marketing-campaigns "Direct link to Start using UTM tags in your marketing campaigns")

To minimize the amount of traffic that falls within the "direct / none" referral source, you can add special query parameters to your links in social media, email newsletters and your paid advertising campaigns.

`ref`, `source`, `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, `utm_campaign`, `utm_content` and `utm_term` query parameters are all valid and supported by Plausible Analytics. [Learn more here](https://plausible.io/docs/top-referrers).

You'll then be able to follow the visitor journey from a paid ad click to a conversion on your site directly in your Plausible dashboard. All the UTM tagged clicks will be displayed in the Campaign tabs of your Top Sources report and all the conversions will be in the Goal Conversions section. You can then [filter your Plausible dashboard](https://plausible.io/docs/filters-segments) by a specific goal to see conversion rate for all the individual sources of traffic, landing pages and so on.

Read also our guide on [how to use UTM parameters to track your campaigns and understand the dark traffic](https://plausible.io/blog/utm-tracking-tags).

### Integrate with Search Console to get keyword phrases people find your site with[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#integrate-with-search-console-to-get-keyword-phrases-people-find-your-site-with "Direct link to Integrate with Search Console to get keyword phrases people find your site with")

You can enable the Google Search Console integration to get all the search keywords people find your site with in Google's search results directly in your Plausible dashboard. [See instructions here](https://plausible.io/docs/google-search-console-integration).

### Opt out from counting your own visits[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#opt-out-from-counting-your-own-visits "Direct link to Opt out from counting your own visits")

When you're working on your site, you might not want to record your own visits and page views. Here's [how to opt out and exclude yourself from your analytics](https://plausible.io/docs/excluding).

### You can proxy our script[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#you-can-proxy-our-script "Direct link to You can proxy our script")

If you'd like to serve the script as a first-party connection from your domain name, we offer a way to proxy our script. See [full details here](https://plausible.io/docs/proxy/introduction).

Reports, notifications and sharing[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#reports-notifications-and-sharing "Direct link to Reports, notifications and sharing")
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Enable email and Slack reports and notifications for traffic spikes[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#enable-email-and-slack-reports-and-notifications-for-traffic-spikes "Direct link to Enable email and Slack reports and notifications for traffic spikes")

You can decide to enable weekly (every Monday) and/or monthly (every 1st day of the month) reports to be sent directly through email. [See instructions here](https://plausible.io/docs/email-reports).

You can also enable traffic spike notifications to be sent directly through email. This will help notify you when your site has an unusually high number of current visitors. [See instructions here](https://plausible.io/docs/traffic-spikes).

And you can get the same reports and traffic spike notifications in Slack too. [See instructions here](https://plausible.io/docs/slack-reports).

You can share your Plausible stats with (potential) partners or advertisers. Or if you're working for an agency or working as a freelancer, you can share the site stats with your clients by generating a shared private link. The shared link is secure and impossible to guess by default. You can even password protect it. You can also limit a shared link to a specific audience segment so viewers only see the data that's relevant to them. [Learn more here](https://plausible.io/docs/shared-links).

You can also choose to be transparent and make your site stats public so anyone with your stats dashboard link can view them. [Learn here how](https://plausible.io/docs/visibility). You can also [embed our default dashboard](https://plausible.io/docs/embed-dashboard) without any Plausible branding directly into your own website.

Collaboration and multi-site management[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#collaboration-and-multi-site-management "Direct link to Collaboration and multi-site management")
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Invite team members and other collaborators[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#invite-team-members-and-other-collaborators "Direct link to Invite team members and other collaborators")

Plausible makes it easy to invite team members, clients and other collaborators. You can invite team members and also assign their roles. You can choose to give people access to the stats dashboard only or give them the permission to edit site settings and invite other collaborators.

You can learn more about [user accounts and levels](https://plausible.io/docs/users-roles).

### Consolidated view for multiple sites[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#consolidated-view-for-multiple-sites "Direct link to Consolidated view for multiple sites")

If you manage multiple websites, the consolidated view gives you an overview of traffic across all of them in a single dashboard. It requires no configuration and automatically updates as you add or remove sites. [Learn more here](https://plausible.io/docs/consolidated-views).

API and data portability[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#api-and-data-portability "Direct link to API and data portability")
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Explore our API[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#explore-our-api "Direct link to Explore our API")

We have a [stats API](https://plausible.io/docs/stats-api) that allows you to take individual metrics and create a custom-built dashboard within your UI in whichever shape or form you want.

We also have a [sites API](https://plausible.io/docs/sites-api) that allows you to add and manage a large number of sites programmatically.

There's also the [events API](https://plausible.io/docs/events-api) that can be used to record pageviews and custom events without JavaScript. This is useful when tracking Android or iOS mobile apps, or for server side tracking.

### Import your historical Google Analytics stats[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#import-your-historical-google-analytics-stats "Direct link to Import your historical Google Analytics stats")

You can import your historical Google Analytics stats into your Plausible dashboard. See [how our GA importer works](https://plausible.io/docs/google-analytics-import).

Explore the intuitive stats dashboard[​](https://plausible.io/docs/your-plausible-experience#explore-the-intuitive-stats-dashboard "Direct link to Explore the intuitive stats dashboard")
